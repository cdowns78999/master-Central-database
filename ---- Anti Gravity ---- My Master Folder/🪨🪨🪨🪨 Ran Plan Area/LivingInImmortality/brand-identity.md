# Living in Immortality — Brand Identity Document

---

## 1. Label Identity

**Full Name:** LIVING IN IMMORTALITY

**Abbreviations / Name Treatments:**
- `LiI` — standard abbreviated form
- `L∞I` — symbolic variant (infinity mark signals eternal loop between the L and I)
- `[living in immortality]` — lowercase bracketed treatment for editorial, small-format, and typographic use

**Type:** VRChat Record Label

**Positioning Statement:**
LIVING IN IMMORTALITY is the first VRChat-native record label built with a real brand, a signed roster, and a world-as-album release model. We exist at the intersection of post-physical space and sonic permanence — where the music is the world, the world is the release, and the artist is never bound by a body or a platform. This is not a label that streams into the void. This is a label that *lives inside it.*

## 2. Taglines

**Primary:**
> "Where the frequency outlives the body."

This one carries the full weight of the name. It speaks directly to the immortality premise without being melodramatic — it's clinical and cosmic at the same time. It works on a label drop, a world load screen, a promo card, a bio footer.

**Secondary:**
> "Born in the static. Alive in the signal."

Origin story energy. Gritty and digital, with a transmission metaphor that maps cleanly onto VRChat's nature as a medium. Works for individual artist launches, teasers, and label intro sequences.

## 4. Typography Direction

**Display / Title Treatment:**
Wide-spaced, ultra-light to thin weight sans-serif or geometric typeface. Letters should feel like they're drifting apart in low gravity. Tracking pushed outward (generous letter spacing). All-caps for the full label name. No decorative serifs — the geometry is the statement.

**Body / Editorial:**
A clean, slightly condensed sans-serif at regular weight. Clinical but not corporate — the kind of type you'd find in an observatory report or an academic paper on dark matter. No humanist warmth. Precision only.

**Accent / Symbol Treatment:**
Monospaced or code-adjacent type for bracketed treatments (`[living in immortality]`), timestamps, release codes, and catalog numbers. Signals transmission, data, and system-origin rather than human origin.

**Key Principle:**
Typography should feel like it was designed for screens that don't exist yet. Avoid anything that reads as vintage, handmade, or warm. The aesthetic is post-physical — refined absence, not romantic decay.

## 5. Logo Concept

The primary mark is the text `L∞I` — three characters treated as a single glyph.

The two `I` characters are rendered identically, anchoring both ends in vertical silence. The infinity symbol `∞` sits at center, scaled to match the cap height of the letters — it functions not as a symbol but as a structural letter replacement, occupying the same visual space a central character would. The result reads as a word, not an equation.

The full wordmark beneath it — `LIVING IN IMMORTALITY` — runs in ultra-light, widely tracked all-caps, sized so the full phrase is roughly the same total width as the `L∞I` mark above it. This creates a stacked lockup: the symbol on top, the name clarifying below.

Color: Signal White on Void Black as the primary treatment. A secondary Nebula Violet glow version (subtle outer radiance on the `∞`) is used for VRChat world signage and animated environments.

The logo should never appear on a white or light background. It lives in dark space only.

## 6. Brand Voice

**Adjectives:** Oracular. Spare. Inevitable. Cinematic.

The label does not explain itself. It declares. Copy is short, weighted, and arrives with the confidence of something that has already outlasted everything else in the room. There is no hype language, no exclamation points, no urgency theater — because nothing this permanent needs to rush.

When LIVING IN IMMORTALITY speaks, it speaks the way a signal sounds after it has traveled ten thousand years to reach you: clear, cold, and absolutely certain it would arrive.

Artist bios, release notes, and world descriptions should read like liner notes found inside a decommissioned space station — factual about strange things, reverent without being sentimental.

## 7. Artist Pitch Angle

**The pitch, direct:**

> You've been building in VRChat because it's the only space that lets you make something truly immersive — a world that IS the music, not just a place where music plays. But the infrastructure has never matched the vision. No label. No brand. No framework that treats what you're doing as a real release with real permanence.
>
> LIVING IN IMMORTALITY is the first label built specifically for this. We sign artists, we build the world alongside you, and we release it as a single unified artifact — the album *is* the world. Your name goes on a label with a real visual identity, a real roster, and a release model that doesn't exist anywhere else.
>
> The lane is completely open. There is no one else doing this with any seriousness. The artists who sign here first are the ones who define what VRChat music culture looks like when it grows up. That's not a marketing line — it's just what's true.

**Pitch frame for occult/ritualistic artists:** Lead with the immortality concept. The label name is a philosophy, not just branding. The work you make here doesn't die when the platform changes — it gets archived, documented, and treated as a permanent cultural artifact.

**Pitch frame for ambient/ethereal wave artists:** Lead with the world-as-album model. The music and the environment are one release. Your sonic world becomes a literal world people can inhabit. This is the format you've always needed.

**Pitch frame for Berlin techno / witch house artists:** Lead with the visual identity and brand seriousness. This is the first VRC label that looks and operates like a real label. If you've been waiting for infrastructure that matches the weight of what you're making — this is it.
