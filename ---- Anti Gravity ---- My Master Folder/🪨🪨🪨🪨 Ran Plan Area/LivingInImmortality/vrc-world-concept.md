# Living in Immortality (LiI) — VRChat World Concept
### Production Brief v1.0 | April 2026

---

## Overview

Living in Immortality is not a venue. It is a label that exists as a place.

This document defines the VRChat world strategy for LiI — a persistent, evolving virtual space where the label's brand, its artists, and its fans share the same air. Every structural decision below is grounded in what VRC's platform can actually do today, and positioned around the gaps nobody in the music industry has filled yet: a real label brand in VR, an artist development pipeline with in-world infrastructure, a music store, and a fan loyalty system that compounds over time.

---

## 1. World Name + Concept

**World Name:** `Living in Immortality — The Label`

**Tagline:** *"The building that never closes."*

The vibe is a brutalist-luxe vertical compound — think midnight concrete and amber light. It reads somewhere between a high-end recording complex, an after-hours archive, and a living monument. The architecture communicates permanence: this label has been here, is here now, and will be here after you leave.

It is not a festival. It is not a club. It is a headquarters.

The world communicates immediately that LiI is a real creative institution — not a bedroom producer with a VRC room. Every zone, surface, and ambient detail is in service of that. New artists feel the weight of the thing when they step in. Fans feel like they belong to something with gravity.

The world updates over time — new releases physically change the space. A new zone opens. A hallway gets repainted. A door that was locked is now open. The world has memory.

## 2. Entry Experience — The First 10 Seconds

You spawn in **The Atrium**.

It is a wide, high-ceilinged entry hall. The floor is polished dark stone. The walls carry the LiI logo — large, clean, lit from below in amber. Ahead of you, a curved reception desk with floating artist cards rotating in slow orbit above it. Soft ambient music plays — a continuous LiI-curated mix that shifts based on time of day (morning: chill, late night: heavier).

Three paths branch from The Atrium:

- **Left** — The Main Stage corridor (large double doors, always lit if a show is live)
- **Center** — Artist Halls and Fan Lounge
- **Right** — The Archive Vault (members only, door visually locked for guests)

A notification panel on the left wall shows:
- Active events (if any)
- Upcoming show dates
- New releases this week

The group membership indicator is visible from spawn — if you're a LiI Group member, your avatar tag reads the tier. Guests see a subtle ambient prompt: *"Join the LiI Group to unlock restricted floors."*

No loading screen confusion. No empty sky box. You land inside something that has shape and intention.

## 3. Zone Map

### Zone 1 — The Main Stage
The performance core. Capacity-filling floor in front of a wide elevated stage with full lighting rigs and a video wall running artist visuals. TopazChat audio feed streams directly to this zone during live events. The stage is designed for full-body tracked artist performance — the avatar rig reads natural movement cleanly at this scale.

Flanking the stage: standing areas with tiered sight lines. A mezzanine level above for premium guests. The room scales to its moment — quiet and atmospheric between shows, electric during them.

### Zone 2 — Artist Halls
A corridor of individual rooms, one per signed artist. Each room is designed by and for that artist — their aesthetic, their visuals, their current and past releases on display. Think of it as a permanent gallery and listening room hybrid. New LiI signees get their hall built as part of their onboarding. Unsigned artists applying to the label can submit a demo here via an in-world kiosk.

The halls are always open. Fans can walk them at 3am on a Tuesday.

### Zone 3 — The Fan Lounge
A social floor — seating, ambient music, a screen cycling fan-submitted content and label news. This is where the community lives between events. Group members get access to the full lounge. Guests get the entry area only — a clear but non-hostile soft wall.

A leaderboard panel tracks fan attendance streaks, event check-ins, and store purchases. Top fans are visually recognized here.

### Zone 4 — The Archive Vault
Members-only. This is the deep catalog floor — every LiI release, every world-as-album drop, behind-the-scenes footage, unreleased stems, early demos artists have chosen to share. It functions as both a music library and a historical record.

The door is always visible from the atrium. The lock is visible to guests. Friction by design.

Subscription tier members get a dedicated wing inside the Vault — a premium sub-section with the rarest material: unreleased tracks, pre-release listens, and direct artist messages.

### Zone 5 — The VIP Room
Accessed only by VIP ticket or subscription tier. This is a smaller, intimate space adjacent to the main stage — separated by a glass wall that gives a premium angle on the live performance. During shows, the performing artist's full-body tracked avatar is mirrored here as well, meaning VIP attendees get a second, closer performance happening simultaneously.

Post-show, the VIP room stays live for a 30-minute artist hang. No press, no crowd — just the people who showed up right.

## 4. Event Model

### Announcement
Events are announced 7–10 days out across three channels simultaneously: LiI's VRC Group (in-world notification), Discord, and social. Group members get 24-hour early access to reserve VIP slots before they open publicly.

### Doors Open
90 minutes before showtime, The Main Stage zone opens for early arrivals. Ambient pre-show music plays. The fan lounge activates — attendance is logged for the loyalty system. This window is deliberately social; it builds crowd energy before the artist ever steps on stage.

### Instance Structure
VRC worlds cap at approximately 40 people per instance. For major LiI events:

- **Public instances** run in parallel — multiple simultaneous rooms, all receiving the same TopazChat audio feed
- **VIP instance** is a single, controlled room — capped at 20, full-body tracking active, artist present
- A designated **instance host** per public room manages moderation and creates a point of contact for the crowd

The label designates one public instance as "official" for simulcast — this is the room being recorded and broadcast.

### Simulcast
Every show broadcasts live to both Twitch and YouTube. The VRC recording captures the main stage perspective. Simulcast begins at door-open — not showtime — to capture the crowd energy building. A screen inside the world shows the live Twitch chat, creating a feedback loop between the in-world audience and the stream audience.

### Show Flow
1. Doors open — fan lounge activates, attendance logged
2. Artist loads into VIP room 30 minutes before set
3. Main stage lights shift — pre-show visual package begins
4. Artist moves to stage, TopazChat feed goes live
5. Set runs — typically 45–90 minutes
6. Post-show: VIP room stays open 30 minutes, public instances stay live for 15 minutes of cooldown music
7. Recording is clipped and posted to the Archive Vault within 48 hours

## 5. Fan Membership Layer

### VRC Group: LiI Official
The LiI Group is the membership infrastructure. Every fan who joins gets:

- Access to The Fan Lounge (full)
- Reduced-friction entry to events (group members load into the official instance first)
- A persistent member tag visible on their avatar in-world
- Access to group announcements (show dates, drops, label news)

### Group Tiers (via VRC Roles)
| Tier | Name | Access |
|---|---|---|
| Guest | Observer | Atrium + partial lounge |
| Member | LiI Family | Full lounge, Artist Halls, general show access |
| Supporter | Inner Circle | Archive Vault access |
| Subscriber | Immortal | VIP room priority, Vault premium wing, pre-release listens |

### VRC Paid Subscriptions (Beta)
The Immortal tier is gated behind VRC's subscription system — recurring credits in exchange for the highest-tier access. This covers:

- Guaranteed VIP room reservation at every show
- Monthly exclusive content drop in the Vault premium wing
- Direct artist message (one per month, routed through the in-world kiosk)
- Physical merch discount code (delivered via group message)

### Loyalty System
Every interaction inside the world accrues to a fan's loyalty score — visible on the lounge leaderboard:

- Event attendance: +10 pts
- VIP attendance: +25 pts
- Store purchase: +15 pts
- Consecutive weekly visits: streak multiplier
- World-as-album unlock on release day: +20 pts

Top 10 on the leaderboard at month end receive a fan spotlight in the Artist Halls corridor and a direct shoutout in the label's group announcement.

## 6. World-as-Album Release Format

The world-as-album is LiI's flagship release mechanic. A new artist does not drop a link. They drop a world.

### Step-by-Step: First LiI Release as a World

**Step 1 — Artist Onboarding (6–8 weeks pre-release)**
Artist signs to LiI. Their Artist Hall is designed and built in Unity — visual direction, color palette, and ambient audio pulled directly from the upcoming album's aesthetic. Hall is locked/invisible during build.

**Step 2 — Album-World Mapping (4–6 weeks pre-release)**
Each track on the album is mapped to a zone, room, or interactive element in the world. Track 1 might play in the entryway of their hall. Track 4 might only activate when you step into a specific darkened corner. The listener navigates the album spatially, not linearly.

**Step 3 — Archive Vault Pre-Load (2 weeks pre-release)**
The full album is loaded into the Archive Vault behind a locked door. Immortal-tier subscribers get a silent hint in their group message: *"Something's loaded."* No details. Creates real anticipation.

**Step 4 — Teaser Instance (1 week pre-release)**
A preview instance of the artist's hall opens — one room, one track, publicly accessible for 48 hours. Linked on social and in the group. Designed to spread. Fans bring friends into VRC specifically to hear it.

**Step 5 — Release Day: World Goes Live**
On release day at a designated time, three things happen simultaneously:
1. The artist's full hall unlocks in the main world
2. The album drops on all DSPs
3. The Archive Vault door opens for members

The simultaneity is the product. You don't listen to the album then explore the world. They are the same act.

**Step 6 — Release Show (same evening)**
A release show runs that night on the main stage. The artist performs the album start-to-finish with full-body tracking in the VIP room. All instances are live. Simulcast runs to Twitch and YouTube. The show is the launch event, the listening party, and the press moment all at once.

**Step 7 — Persistent Catalog**
The artist's hall stays live indefinitely. It becomes a permanent exhibit in the label. Future fans who find LiI a year later can walk the first release exactly as it was built. The world accumulates history.

## 7. Technical Stack

### Unity + VRChat SDK
All world construction uses the **VRChat World SDK (SDK3)** inside Unity. Udon# scripting handles:
- Zone gating (group membership checks)
- Loyalty point triggers (proximity zones, interaction events)
- Dynamic content panels (rotating artist cards, leaderboard data)
- Door/lock state management tied to group role

World upload and versioning is managed through VRChat's creator portal. Updates to the world (new artist halls, post-release vault unlocks) are deployed as world updates — no new world link needed, same persistent URL.

### TopazChat — Audio
**TopazChat** is used for all live event audio. It provides high-fidelity audio streaming directly inside VRC without the degradation of VRC's default voice system. Setup per show:
- Artist's audio (DAW output or live mix) routes into TopazChat server
- TopazChat stream is ingested by the in-world speaker system via a streaming audio component
- All parallel instances receive the same feed — audio is consistent across every room

### Simulcast — Twitch + YouTube
OBS captures the VRC client on the designated "broadcast instance" machine. Scene setup:
- Primary scene: in-world stage camera angle
- Secondary scene: artist's IRL/avatar cam (for VIP close-up cuts)
- Overlay: LiI branding, track title, live chat ticker

Stream goes to Twitch and YouTube simultaneously via multi-stream output (Restream or OBS dual-output). VODs are downloaded after the stream and uploaded to the Archive Vault within 48 hours.

### VRC Groups + Subscriptions
Group management handles all membership tiers:
- **VRC Groups** = tier roles (Family, Inner Circle, Immortal)
- **VRC Paid Subscriptions** = Immortal tier recurring access (credits-based, beta system)
- Group messages handle event announcements, loyalty milestone notifications, and release alerts
- In-world, SDK3 checks group membership client-side to toggle zone access

### In-World Music Store (Phase 2)
Phase 2 adds an in-world kiosk in The Fan Lounge — interactive panels linking to external purchase (Bandcamp, merch store). VRC does not currently support native transactions, so all commerce is routed through external links surfaced cleanly in-world. The kiosk logs interaction events for loyalty scoring.
