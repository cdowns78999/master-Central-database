# Streaming — Research Findings

## Key Findings

- **Global recorded music revenue reached $31.7B in 2025, up 6.4% YoY, with paid streaming subscribers hitting 837 million** — streaming overall now accounts for roughly 69% of recorded-music trade revenue, and paid subscriptions alone drove most of the 2024 growth (+9.5%). [Source: IFPI Global Music Report 2026 / Music Business Worldwide, 2026]

- **Spotify paid a record $11 billion in royalties in 2025 (lifetime payouts now ~$70B), with 1,500 artists generating over $1M and 13,800+ generating at least $100K on Spotify alone** — but Spotify payouts only grew ~10% YoY, and independent commentators note the per-stream rate kept sliding as total stream volume grew faster than the royalty pool. [Source: Spotify Loud & Clear 2026, March 2026 / Hypebot / Billboard]

- **Per-stream payouts diverge by an order of magnitude across platforms in 2025: Tidal ~$0.013, Apple Music ~$0.007–0.01, Amazon Music ~$0.004–0.008, Spotify ~$0.003–0.005, YouTube Music ~$0.00069** — Apple and Tidal are premium-only, which mechanically lifts their averages versus ad-supported hybrids. [Source: Royalty Exchange, 2025 / Rebel Music Distribution, 2025 / Dynamoi royalty-data analysis]

- **Spotify's 1,000-stream-per-12-months threshold (live since April 2024) demonetized roughly 87% of the catalog at introduction, redirecting ~$1B toward artists above the bar** — Impala and indie-label bodies report disproportionate harm to classical, jazz, regional, and non-English repertoire, with 65% of surveyed labels reporting significant revenue loss. [Source: Spotify for Artists, 2023 / Music Ally, Dec 2025 / Music Business Worldwide, 2024]

- **Deezer's artist-centric model (launched Q4 2023 with UMG, expanded to Merlin in 2024 and SACEM publishing in Jan 2025) removed ~26 million tracks (~13% of catalog) and caps any single user's contribution at 1,000 monthly streams** — 44% of tracks uploaded daily to Deezer are now fully AI-generated (~75,000/day), and Deezer reports up to 85% of streams on fully AI tracks were fraudulent in 2025. [Source: Deezer Newsroom, 2023–2026 / Music Business Worldwide, April 2026 / TechCrunch, April 2026]

- **The top 1% of artists capture roughly 90% of global streams; the top 10% capture ~99.4%** — breaking into the top 1% on Spotify requires ~240,000 monthly listeners, and only ~12,000 artists worldwide exceed 1M monthly listeners. Academic work (Tofalvy & Koltai, 2023) argues Spotify's recommendation system structurally reproduces this inequality rather than diversifying the long tail. [Source: Rolling Stone Pro, 2024 / "Splendid Isolation," Sage/New Media & Society, 2023]

- **Non-major (independent) labels grew 8.2% to $10.7B in 2024, taking 29.7% ownership share; counting self-releasing artists, the "non-major" share is 46.7% of the global market (2023 data)** — but self-releasing "Artists Direct" revenue growth lagged the market ($2.0B) after new royalty thresholds kicked in, even though the number of self-releasing artists grew 17.2%. [Source: MIDiA Research "Recorded Music Market Shares 2024," March 2025 / Billboard Pro]

- **Streaming fraud is now estimated to drain ~$1B annually from the royalty pool; Spotify removed 75M fraudulent tracks in the past year and ~106,000 new tracks hit DSPs daily in 2025** — regulators and DSPs are pushing the industry toward artist-centric / engagement-weighted models in part to neutralize bot-farm economics. [Source: Music Business Worldwide, 2025 / Billboard Pro, 2025 / WIPO Magazine]

## Why It Matters for Artists

The streaming market is still growing, but growth is now concentrated in subscription-pricing power and a shrinking per-stream unit value — so the "earn more by streaming more" playbook only works for artists already above platform monetization thresholds (1,000 streams/track on Spotify, 1,000 streams + 500 unique listeners/month on Deezer). For independent artists, the ground is shifting twice at once: royalty models are being rewritten to favor "professional" and engaged-fan streams (good for niche artists with real fanbases), while AI-generated content and bot-driven streaming inflate the denominator of the pro-rata pool (bad for everyone legit). Long-tail artists who rely on passive algorithmic placements are the most exposed; artists with identifiable superfan audiences are the most structurally advantaged by the model changes now rolling out.

## Artist-Actionable Implications

- **Clear the 1,000-stream monetization bar on each track before promoting the next one.** On Spotify, tracks under 1,000 annual streams earn $0; budget marketing per-release to cross the threshold rather than spreading thin across a large catalog.
- **Prioritize platforms and distribution deals that route to artist-centric pools (Deezer via Merlin, SoundCloud Fan Powered Royalties).** MIDiA's SoundCloud data shows mid-tail artists tend to move into higher income brackets under user-centric/fan-powered models.
- **Treat superfan conversion as a revenue line, not a vibe.** MIDiA estimates roughly 2–3% superfan share of your audience is needed to double income vs. pro-rata — bundle direct-to-fan offers (Bandcamp, paid community tiers, vinyl) on top of streaming.
- **Tag and document human-made work to stay on the safe side of AI-detection sweeps.** Deezer already tags AI-generated music and excludes suspicious streams from royalties; expect Spotify and Apple to add similar filters — metadata hygiene protects your payouts.
- **Diversify off the Spotify unit economic.** Apple Music, Tidal, and Amazon pay 2x–4x per stream; even modest listener migration shifts revenue materially. Pitch for Apple Music editorial, Tidal HiFi features, and Amazon Music follow-ups, not only Spotify playlists.
