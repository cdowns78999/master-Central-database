# Touring — Research Findings

## Key Findings

- **The Eras Tour rewrote the ceiling of live music.** Taylor Swift's Eras Tour closed December 8, 2024 as the first tour in history to cross $2 billion, finishing at $2,077,618,725 and 10,168,008 tickets across 149 shows in 51 stadiums, 19 countries, and 5 continents — per figures reported to Billboard Boxscore; Pollstar's extrapolated estimate is $2.2B and 10.055M tickets ([Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/taylor-swift-eras-tour-earnings-2-billion-sales-1235847513/), [Pollstar](https://news.pollstar.com/2024/12/09/taylor-swifts-eras-tour-sets-all-time-touring-record-breaking-2b/)).

- **Stadium pop is now a two-horse race at the top, with Coldplay setting the sustainability template.** Pollstar's 2024 Year-End named Swift and Coldplay #1 and #2 worldwide; Coldplay's Music of the Spheres is the only other tour to cross $1B and cut per-show direct CO2e emissions 59% vs. their 2016–17 tour (MIT Environmental Solutions Initiative-verified) via a BMW rechargeable show battery, solar/kinetic floors, HVO fuel, 100% biodegradable confetti, and a SAP-powered fan travel app that cut the average fan's footprint by ~50% ([Pollstar 2024 Analysis](https://news.pollstar.com/2024/12/13/2024bizanalysis/), [Variety](https://variety.com/2023/music/news/coldplay-tour-carbon-footprint-sustainability-green-1235632704/), [Coldplay Sustainability](https://sustainability.coldplay.com/)).

- **The Sphere residency model is a new, high-margin revenue lane.** Sphere posted >$420M in 2024 concert revenue from 1.3M tickets — the top venue gross in Billboard Boxscore's 50-year history. U2:UV Achtung Baby grossed $244.5M across 40 shows (663K tickets, avg ticket ~$391, with $600 (RED) Zone seats), Dead & Company did $131.8M / 477K tickets, Eagles $42.2M over the first 8 shows (extended through 2026), and Phish four nights grossed $13.4M / 66.7K. U2 reportedly invested >$20M in custom visual content to play the room ([Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/pro/u2-shows-sphere-110-million-ticket-sales/), [Pollstar / Sphere FY24](https://news.pollstar.com/2024/08/15/sphere-entertainment-revenue-tops-1b-for-fiscal-year/), [Las Vegas Review-Journal / Kats](https://neon.reviewjournal.com/kats/how-sphere-posted-record-breaking-numbers-for-24-3250826/)).

- **2025 normalized downward — ticket sales dropped but average ticket prices rose.** Pollstar's 2025 Year-End ("A Return to Earth") crowned Beyoncé's Cowboy Carter Tour #1 with $407.6M on 1.57M tickets across 32 shows (avg $255.36). Oasis's 36-show reunion was #2 at an estimated $405.4M on 2.23M tickets (avg $181.93), and Coldplay's Music of the Spheres was #3 at $390.5M on 2.91M tickets (avg $133.80) — i.e., Beyoncé beat Oasis by grossing more on fewer tickets via premium pricing ([Pollstar 2025 Analysis](https://news.pollstar.com/2025/12/23/year-end-business-analysis-a-return-to-earth-2025-grosses-ticket-sales-drop-averages-increase-beyonce-oasis-coldplay-have-top-tours-venues-stadiums-rock/), [IQ Magazine](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/12/beyonce-pips-oasis-in-highest-grossing-tour-stakes/)). Beyoncé's 2023 Renaissance World Tour grossed $579M on 2.7M fans / 56 dates and generated a projected $4.5B in broader U.S. economic activity ([Variety](https://variety.com/2023/music/news/beyonces-renaissance-world-tour-revenue-1235743490/)).

- **The Live Nation / Ticketmaster antitrust case reached verdict and settlement in 2026.** DOJ and 40 state AGs filed May 23, 2024, alleging Live Nation controls ~86% of major-venue ticketing and ~78% of large amphitheaters. Trial began March 2026; on April 15, 2026 a federal jury in SDNY found Live Nation violated federal and state antitrust laws. A DOJ settlement includes a $280M fine, a 15% service-fee cap at Live Nation-owned venues, and divestiture of 13 U.S. amphitheaters — but 26 states + DC rejected the terms and are continuing their own suit ([DOJ press release](https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/justice-department-sues-live-nation-ticketmaster-monopolizing-markets-across-live-concert), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/15/live-nation-ticketmaster-lose-antitrust-case), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/09/nx-s1-5742433/live-nation-ticketmaster-doj-antitrust-case)).

- **The market below the top line is a Live Nation–AEG duopoly, but Live Nation dwarfs AEG ~5:1.** In 2023–24 Live Nation sold ~40M tickets worldwide vs. AEG Presents' ~8.2M (Statista). AEG's own CEO Jay Marciano publicly called Live Nation/Ticketmaster a monopoly in 2024. AEG is more venue/real-estate heavy; Live Nation layers promotion, ticketing (Ticketmaster), venue ownership, and artist management (>500 artists) in one stack ([Variety](https://variety.com/2024/music/news/ticketmaster-live-nation-monopoly-aeg-chief-jay-marciano-says-1236021356/), [Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/304982/leading-music-promoters-worldwide/)).

- **For developing artists, touring is still mostly a money-losing proposition.** Only ~57% of indie touring musicians turned a profit post-pandemic, and the ones who did averaged ~$3,800 net ([Side Door](https://news.sidedooraccess.com/news/touring-by-the-numbers)). Vans remain the default — bus pricing has risen "astronomically" — and a 2022–23 crew/driver shortage forced tour managers to lock crew and vehicles 6 months ahead vs. days ahead pre-pandemic; 69% of live-event companies still report shortfalls in engineers, techs, crew, and riggers ([Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/pro/how-artists-tour-budget-stolen-vans-ancient-gear-livable-basics/), [GRAMMY.com](https://www.grammy.com/news/2023-post-pandemic-touring-changes-festivals-costs-personnel-backstage-behind-the-scenes)).

- **Brexit has structurally damaged UK artists' ability to tour the EU.** UK artists touring the EU in summer 2023 were down 32% vs. 2017–19, and the Independent Society of Musicians found ~50% of UK music pros reported declines in EU work, with ~30% reporting no work at all since Brexit. The 90-in-180-day Schengen limit knocks crew out mid-tour; UK-based trucking, which used to hold ~85% of European music haulage, is being rebuilt in Amsterdam and Dublin ([NME](https://www.nme.com/news/music/brexit-touring-europe-strangling-talent-carnets-visa-merch-crew-government-response-summer-2022-3288735), [UK Music](https://www.ukmusic.org/policy-campaigns/brexit/)).

## Why It Matters for Artists

Touring — not recorded music — is where the overwhelming majority of modern artist income now lives, but access to that money is gated by who owns the venue, the ticketing pipe, the promoter, and the crew. The 2023–25 era revealed a barbell: billion-dollar stadium and Sphere-scale tours are printing historic grosses for ~30 superstars, while developing acts face shrinking margins from van/bus inflation, crew shortages, and (if UK-based) Brexit friction — all while the same company (Live Nation) effectively sits on both sides of the table. For independent artists specifically, the DOJ settlement's 15% venue-fee cap and amphitheater divestitures could meaningfully reshape routing economics over the next 3–5 years.

## Artist-Actionable Implications

- **Route around the duopoly when you can.** Independent promoters, AEG-operated rooms, and non-Live-Nation regional venues will become more price-competitive as the DOJ divestitures (13 amphitheaters) and 15% fee cap roll in — track which venues on a tour route are covered.
- **Stay in the van longer than your ego wants.** The economic reality is clear: ~57% of indie tours turn a profit and only by ~$3,800 avg; bus upgrade decisions should wait until guarantees + merch reliably clear the delta. Cluster 3–4 shows around a central Airbnb, and book crew/vehicles ≥6 months out because post-pandemic availability is still tight.
- **Negotiate tour support as a tour-income-only recoupment, not a cross-collateralized advance.** Label tour support is standard recoupable expense (typical development-deal advance: $50K–$350K), but in 360-deal structures it can recoup against record royalties AND touring income — insist in writing that tour support only recoups from tour revenue.
- **Treat sustainability as a promoter/sponsor unlock, not just a moral one.** Coldplay's 59% emissions cut was co-financed by BMW, SAP, and DHL partnerships — the Music of the Spheres model shows that a credible, MIT-verified climate plan unlocks corporate sponsorship dollars that offset production costs.
- **UK-based? Build EU dates with a visa/carnet budget line-item from day one, and consider basing your trucking partner in Dublin or Amsterdam.** The 90/180 Schengen cap means crews that stack back-to-back EU tours will run out of days — rotating UK + EU crew or contracting EU-based backline is now a structural, not cosmetic, part of routing.
