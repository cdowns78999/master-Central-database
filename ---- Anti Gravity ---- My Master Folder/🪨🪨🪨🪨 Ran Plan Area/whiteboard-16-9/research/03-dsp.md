# DSP — Research Findings

## Key Findings

- **Spotify dominates a top-heavy global market.** The top five DSPs — Spotify, Tencent Music Entertainment, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and YouTube Music — collectively held roughly 72% of global music subscription revenue in 2024, with Spotify alone at ~32.2% share. Spotify added 28 million subscribers in 2024, more than Tencent, Apple, and Amazon combined, and YouTube Music was the fastest-growing DSP by percentage, nearly displacing Apple from 4th place ([MIDiA Research, 2025](https://www.midiaresearch.com/blog/music-subscriber-market-shares-2024-slowdown-what-slowdown)).

- **Spotify's 1,000-stream threshold redirected an estimated $47M away from small artists in 2024.** Starting April 2024, tracks must accumulate at least 1,000 streams in the prior 12 months to earn from the recorded-music royalty pool. Spotify says sub-threshold tracks represent only ~0.5% of total streams while 87% of all ~202M tracks on the platform fall below the line. Luminate data cited by Disc Makers CEO Tony van Veen pegs the 2024 reallocation at ~$46.9M that would have otherwise gone to emerging artists ([Music Ally, 2025](https://musically.com/2025/12/16/spotify-defends-1000-stream-royalties-threshold-after-critical-report/); [Spotify for Artists, 2024](https://artists.spotify.com/en/blog/modernizing-our-royalty-system)).

- **Spotify's recommender is a multi-signal stack, fronted by BaRT.** BaRT ("Bandits for Recommendations as Treatments") — introduced in Spotify's 2018 paper *Explore, Exploit, Explain* — uses contextual multi-armed bandits to decide what populates Home, weighing a 30-second listen as a positive signal. It fuses collaborative filtering (user-behavior similarity), NLP over web text/metadata, and raw-audio content analysis so brand-new tracks with no behavioral history can still surface ([Spotify Research, 2018](https://research.atspotify.com/publications/explore-exploit-explain-personalizing-explainable-recommendations-with-bandits/); [Music Tomorrow, 2025](https://www.music-tomorrow.com/blog/how-spotify-recommendation-system-works-complete-guide)).

- **Editorial pitches feed algorithmic playlists even when rejected.** Spotify for Artists requires a pitch 7+ days pre-release; submissions made 14+ days early see roughly 2x editorial consideration, and historically ~1 in 5 pitched tracks land on at least one editorial list. Pitch metadata (genre, mood, instruments) is used by the algorithm regardless of editorial outcome, and engagement in the first 48–72 hours drives Release Radar and Discover Weekly placement ([Spotify for Artists, 2024](https://support.spotify.com/us/artists/article/pitching-music-to-playlist-editors/); [Chartlex, 2026](https://www.chartlex.com/blog/streaming/how-to-pitch-to-spotify-playlists-2026-step-by-step-guide)).

- **Independents now capture ~50% of the Spotify royalty pool.** Loud & Clear 2025/2026 reports Spotify paid out a record $11B to rights-holders in 2025 (up from $10B in 2024). Independent artists and labels generated roughly half of total Spotify royalties, with ~13,800 artists earning $100K+ and ~1,500 earning $1M+ from Spotify alone in 2025. More than a third of artists earning $10K+ are DIY or started DIY ([Spotify Newsroom, 2026](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2026-03-11/loud-and-clear-music-economics-highlights/); [Music Week, 2025](https://www.musicweek.com/digital/read/spotify-loud-clear-2025-report-breaks-down-its-10-billion-role-in-the-streaming-economy/091558)).

- **Spotify purged 75M+ "spammy" tracks in the 12 months leading to Sept 2025, and adopted DDEX AI disclosures.** The September 2025 policy bundle added an impersonation ban (vocal cloning only with consent), a spam filter that tags and down-ranks abusive uploaders, and a standardized DDEX field for AI-usage disclosure in credits. Spotify explicitly avoids automated AI-detection as an enforcement tool, citing false-positive rates ([Music Business Worldwide, 2025](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/spotify-has-deleted-75m-spammy-tracks-as-it-unveils-new-ai-music-policies/); [Spotify Newsroom, 2025](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2025-09-25/spotify-strengthens-ai-protections/)).

- **Regional DSPs still own their home markets.** Tencent Music Entertainment holds ~72% of China's streaming market by user volume with 124.4M paying subs (Q2 2025, $22B RMB subscription run-rate, +28% YoY); NetEase Cloud Music has ~218M MAU and ~50M paying subs. Anghami grew MENA premium accounts +18% YoY via Arabic podcast tie-ins and telco bundling, and MENA streaming revenue jumped +22.8% in 2024. India's market is now dominated by Spotify (~26% share) and YouTube Music, with JioSaavn and Gaana as the only remaining domestic players after a wave of shut-downs ([Variety, 2025](https://variety.com/2025/music/news/tencent-music-q3-revenue-2025-1236577827/); [Music Ally, 2025](https://musically.com/2025/04/02/shut-downs-watching-vs-listening-and-future-growth-indias-dsp-market-today/); [Mordor Intelligence, 2025](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/music-streaming-market)).

## Why It Matters for Artists

DSPs are no longer neutral pipes — they are curatorial gatekeepers whose algorithms decide whether a release is heard at all, and whose policy levers (stream thresholds, AI filters, editorial pitching windows) can add or subtract meaningful income overnight. The pro-rata pool model means every artist is competing for a slice of the same royalty pie, so velocity in the first 72 hours of a release and consistent engagement signals are worth more than absolute stream counts. Platform diversification — especially into YouTube Music, Tencent, Anghami, Boomplay, and JioSaavn — is increasingly a revenue strategy, not a vanity metric, because subscriber growth is now 78% outside North America and Europe.

## Artist-Actionable Implications

- **Pitch to Spotify for Artists at least 14 days early** with complete metadata (genre, mood, instruments, cultural tags) — this roughly doubles editorial consideration and also trains Release Radar/Discover Weekly even if no editor picks the track ([Chartlex, 2026](https://www.chartlex.com/blog/streaming/how-to-pitch-to-spotify-playlists-2026-step-by-step-guide)).
- **Front-load promotion to cross 1,000 streams per track within 12 months.** Run pre-save campaigns 3–4 weeks pre-release and concentrate ad spend in the first 48–72 hours — that window is where up to 70% of week-one listening and all algorithmic signal accumulation happens ([Artistrack, 2026](https://artistrack.com/spotify-pre-save-campaign-guide/)).
- **Treat BaRT's 30-second mark as the real success metric.** A skip before 30 seconds is a negative signal; prioritize hook-forward intros, strong first-10-seconds retention, and monitor Spotify for Artists' "Listeners that skipped" data per track ([Spotify Research, 2018](https://research.atspotify.com/publications/explore-exploit-explain-personalizing-explainable-recommendations-with-bandits/)).
- **Disclose any AI usage via your distributor's DDEX fields** to stay ahead of Spotify's 2025 transparency rules and avoid spam-filter tagging, which silently down-ranks offenders ([Spotify Newsroom, 2025](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2025-09-25/spotify-strengthens-ai-protections/)).
- **Distribute beyond the big three.** At per-stream rates of $0.003–$0.005 on Spotify, regional DSPs (Anghami, Boomplay, JioSaavn, Tencent sub-platforms) and YouTube Music's content-aware recommender can materially add to royalties — especially since YouTube Music is the fastest-growing global DSP and uses different signals (tempo, instrumentation, cross-platform video behavior) that may surface catalog Spotify misses ([MIDiA Research, 2025](https://www.midiaresearch.com/blog/music-subscriber-market-shares-2024-slowdown-what-slowdown); [Music Tomorrow, 2025](https://www.music-tomorrow.com/blog/a-complete-guide-to-youtube-recommendation-algorithms-for-music-and-artists)).
