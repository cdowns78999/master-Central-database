II.

Biography & Press

A voice like lamplight through a dusty window. A pen like a confession nobody asked for.

Born in a river town whose name she rarely shares, Lenore Ashworth writes songs that sound like rooms nobody has opened in a hundred years. Her work draws from Victorian mourning culture, slow-burn folk, and the liminal hush of cathedrals after everyone has gone home.

Since her 2017 debut Vesper, she has performed at the Sacred Sounds Festival (Prague), the Old Operating Theatre (London), and a handful of candlelit churches across the American South.
