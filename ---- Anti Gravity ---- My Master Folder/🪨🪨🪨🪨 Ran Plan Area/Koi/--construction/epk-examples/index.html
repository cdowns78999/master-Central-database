Five inventive EPKs

The mould-breakers. The EPKs a booking agent screenshots and sends to their team.

1

Chelsea Wolfe

Fullscreen gothic web experience

The inventive move: Black-velvet loading screen, candle-flicker cursor, parallax stained glass, and press quotes engraved as gravestone text with the EPK PDF styled as a funeral program.

Why it works: Every UI element is a ritual object — the EPK performs the aesthetic instead of describing it.

Steal for KoiReplace the cursor with a flickering candle flame and render press quotes in engraved-stone typography inside a stained-glass parallax frame.
2

Diamanda Galás

Occult-library anti-UX

The inventive move: Refuses modern web conventions entirely, presenting as a 1999 occult library with press clippings shown as yellowed, scanned archival pages.

Why it works: The "broken" feel becomes the brand — it signals authenticity, age, and uncompromising vision before a single word is read.

Steal for KoiScan real paper press clippings with visible foxing and tape marks, then lay them on parchment with no grid — let the archival mess be the design.
3

Bat for Lashes — "Lost Girls"

Fake-world booking hotline

The inventive move: Entire site staged as 80s horror VHS tape — tracking lines, tabloid clippings from a fictional town, and a booking line disguised as a small-town hotline number.

Why it works: It builds a narrative universe the artist lives inside; press and booking feel like artifacts from a story rather than business assets.

Steal for KoiFrame booking as a Victorian calling-card / telegram office — fictional establishment name, filigree border, "hours of correspondence."
4

Björk — Biophilia

Album-as-app EPK

The inventive move: The album is an iOS app where each song is its own interactive universe — no "listen here" button, the user physically conducts the music.

Why it works: Destroys the passive EPK page entirely; press and music discovery become one ritualised act of participation.

Steal for KoiMake the landing a "music box" or lit chandelier the visitor must interact with (light candles, turn a key) to unlock each section.
5

FKA twigs — Caprisongs

Tarot-pull song assignment

The inventive move: Visitors pull a tarot card that assigns them a song, with the artist's voice-notes bleeding through the site as ambient texture.

Why it works: Turns the EPK into a divinatory ritual — personal, theatrical, and shareable without feeling like a press pitch.

Steal for KoiOpen with a Victorian tarot / oracle-card draw that routes visitors to a personalised "dossier" — their press kit, their track, their show.

Five patterns to steal for Koi

The abstracted moves. Extract the craft, not the reference — these are the levers to pull.

1. Era-Locked Visual Chapters

the moveTreat each release or body of work as its own sub-page with dedicated typography, palette, and photo set — not one flat gallery.
why it fits koiOpera thinks in acts and librettos; chaptered eras let each production carry its own Victorian mood (mourning jet, candlelit gold, bone-white bridal) without diluting the whole.
Build /eras/[name] per release with its own CSS-variables block overriding base tokens (font-family, accent color, photo grid).

2. Artifact-as-Document Press Pack

the movePresent the downloadable EPK not as a clean corporate PDF but as an in-world artifact — a funeral program, playbill, or engraved broadsheet.
why it fits koiA Victorian-goth opera artist's entire brand promise is theatrical object-hood; a booker's download should feel like they just pulled a program from the opera-house floor.
Export the PDF with serif display type, deckle-edge PNG borders, and a cover styled as a 19th-century opera playbill. Link it as "Program" — not "EPK."

3. Engraved Pull-Quote Treatment

the moveRender press quotes as typeset inscriptions (gravestone, plaque, program epigraph) rather than a logo wall or plain blockquote.
why it fits koiGothic and operatic traditions revere engraved text — it turns critic praise into monument, matching the artist's gravity instead of fighting it.
Build a .quote-plaque component: centered serif caps, thin double-rule border, tracked-out outlet name, subtle stone / parchment background-image.

4. Atmospheric Cursor + Parallax Skin

the moveLayer one small ambient interaction (cursor trail, candle flicker, veil parallax) over an otherwise restrained layout so the site feels haunted without losing usability.
why it fits koiOpera stagecraft is built on atmosphere cues — a single flickering element delivers theatricality without turning the EPK into a game.
Fixed-position SVG candle flame following mousemove with filter: blur() + subtle @keyframes flicker, and a background image layer moving at 0.3× scroll speed.

5. Bio as Libretto, Not Resume

the moveWrite the long bio in first-person lyrical or third-person editorial prose — closer to a program note or short story than a career summary.
why it fits koiAn operatic artist's origin should read like the opening of an aria's libretto; a pitch-deck bio undercuts the entire persona.
Split the bio into a short epigraph (italic, centered), a 3-paragraph editorial body in Cormorant or EB Garamond, and a separate "Facts" sidebar for the resume data bookers need.
