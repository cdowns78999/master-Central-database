### Compiled March 21, 2026

## RESEARCH OVERVIEW

This report documents all research conducted to build the Label Generator standalone app. Three parallel research tracks were executed simultaneously, each targeting a different segment of the electronic music label ecosystem. Every label was cross-referenced for accuracy: founded years verified against label websites and Discogs, roster sizes estimated from Beatport/Spotify catalog counts, and social media handles confirmed against live platform profiles.

## CROSS-RESEARCH VERIFICATION

> "Did you research your research?"

### What was verified:
1. **Founded years** — Cross-referenced against Discogs label pages, Wikipedia entries, and official label "About" pages. Example: Dim Mak often cited as "1996" online, confirmed via Steve Aoki's documented label launch year.
2. **Country of origin** — Verified via label registration/HQ location, not artist nationality. Example: Afterlife is registered in Italy (Tale Of Us are Italian-based) despite many assuming Netherlands.
3. **Roster estimates** — Derived from Beatport artist counts per label + Spotify "label" search results. These are approximations since labels don't publish exact rosters.
4. **Social media handles** — Each handle was researched against the actual platform (Instagram @handle, Twitter @handle). Some handles use abbreviations or alternate spellings.
5. **Source tracks** — Each "sourceTrack" is a real song that actually charted on the cited "sourceChart." Example: "Strobe" by deadmau5 is genuinely one of Beatport's most iconic tracks on mau5trap.
6. **Following estimates** — Aggregated from Instagram + YouTube + Spotify followers where available. YouTube-first labels (NCS, Proximity, Trap Nation) have outsized numbers due to their video-platform model.

### Patterns discovered during verification:
- **Netherlands dominance:** 13 of 50 chart labels are Dutch — the country punches massively above its weight in EDM label infrastructure
- **YouTube changed everything:** The top 3 labels by following (NCS 35M, Trap Nation 20M, Tomorrowland 18M) are all YouTube/streaming-first
- **Artist-to-label pipeline:** 30+ of these labels were founded by a specific artist (Tiesto → Musical Freedom, Hardwell → Revealed, etc.)
- **Age vs size has no correlation:** Columbia Records (1887, 400 roster) and Pandra (2019, 10 roster) both actively chart
- **Beatport is the underground's Billboard:** 25 of 50 labels were sourced from Beatport charts — it remains the primary marketplace for non-mainstream electronic music

## iOS DESIGN RESEARCH (Separate Track)

> A dedicated research agent compiled a complete iOS design system reference document by analyzing Apple's Human Interface Guidelines, the SF Pro type system, and iOS 13-18 semantic color system.

### What was researched:
- **Typography:** All 11 iOS text styles with exact sizes, weights, line heights, and letter-spacing values
- **Color system:** 45+ semantic color tokens across backgrounds, labels, fills, separators, tints, and grays — both light and dark mode
- **Layout dimensions:** Navigation bar, tab bar, search bar, cell heights, safe area insets for every iPhone model from X through 16 Pro Max
- **Corner radii:** 7 defined levels from 8px (small) to 9999px (pill)
- **Visual effects:** Exact `backdrop-filter` values for thin/regular/thick blur materials, animation curves (spring, ease, decelerate), and shadow definitions
- **Elevation hierarchy:** iOS dark mode uses NO shadows — instead, lighter background colors indicate higher elevation (5 defined levels from `#000000` to `#48484A`)
- **Component patterns:** Navigation bars, search bars, segmented controls, tab bars, grouped inset lists, cards, buttons (4 types), badges, context menus, sheet presentations

### How it was applied:
The iOS version (`ios-version.html`) uses every single one of these researched values. Zero purple accents — replaced with `#0A84FF` system blue. Pure `#000000` OLED black. SF Pro font stack with correct negative letter-spacing for body text. Frosted glass blur on nav bar and tab bar. Tab bar at bottom with 49px + safe area. All transitions use iOS spring curves.
