### 1. MAYNARD JAMES KEENAN
**Role:** Lead Vocalist — Tool (1990–present), A Perfect Circle (1999–present), Puscifer (2003–present)

**Full Name:** James Herbert Keenan
**Born:** April 17, 1964 — Akron, Ohio

**Bio:**
James Herbert Keenan grew up in Ohio and Michigan, enlisting in the U.S. Army before attending West Point Prep School (1983–1984), ultimately declining an appointment to the Academy to pursue art. He studied at Kendall College of Art and Design in Grand Rapids, Michigan, arriving in Los Angeles in the late 1980s where he met Adam Jones and co-founded Tool in 1990. His mother's 1976 aneurysm — which left her paralyzed for the remaining 27 years of her life — became one of the central emotional engines of his songwriting, most explicitly addressed on *10,000 Days*' title suite. Beyond music, Keenan established Caduceus Cellars (founded 2004) and Merkin Vineyards in Jerome and the Cornville/Page Springs area of Arizona's Verde Valley, structuring his touring commitments around harvest and bottling seasons. He published a memoir, *A Perfect Union of Contrary Things*, in 2016, tracing his life from Ohio through military service, art school, and the building of Tool.

**Key Contributions to Tool:**
- Primary lyricist and sole vocalist across all five studio albums
- Philosophical and narrative architecture of albums — introspective, mythological, often directly autobiographical
- Served as producer or co-producer on several releases
- Established the band's performance mystique: frequently performs with his back to the audience, surrounded by low lighting

**Side Projects / Other Work:**
- **A Perfect Circle** (1999–present): Co-founded with guitarist Billy Howerdel; Keenan describes it as more "right-brain feminine" relative to Tool's "left-brain masculine" quality. Debut *Mer de Noms* (2000) sold 188,000 copies first week — highest-selling debut by a rock band at that time.
- **Puscifer** (2003–present): Self-described "playground for the various voices in my head" — blends electronic, art rock, post-industrial; five albums through 2026.
- **Caduceus Cellars / Merkin Vineyards**: Nationally distributed Arizona winery. A wine called "Nagual del Judith" — 100% Arizona Cabernet Sauvignon — is dedicated to his late mother, whose ashes are scattered at the vineyard.
- **Film:** Subject of *Blood Into Wine* (2010), documentary on the Arizona wine project.

### 2. ADAM JONES
**Role:** Lead Guitarist, Music Video Director, Visual Art Director — Tool (1990–present)

**Full Name:** Adam Thomas Jones
**Born:** January 15, 1965

**Bio:**
Before forming Tool, Adam Jones built a significant career in Hollywood's special effects industry, training at Stan Winston's workshop and Rick Lazzarini's Character Shop and contributing to landmark productions including *Jurassic Park*, *Terminator 2: Judgment Day*, *Batman Returns*, and the *A Nightmare on Elm Street* series. He brought that background in physical fabrication, stop-motion, and sculptural design directly into Tool's visual identity, directing the majority of the band's music videos using 3D clay techniques and stop-motion animation he had honed in film work. As art director on *10,000 Days* (2006), Jones won the Grammy Award for Best Recording Package — the album's packaging featured Alex Grey artwork viewable through stereoscopic 3D glasses built into the case. His guitar playing is characterized by a deliberate eclecticism: "alternately utilizing power chords, scratchy noise, chiming arpeggios, off-beat rhythm patterns, and a quiet minimalism," often using non-traditional objects as plectrums and maintaining an extensive live effects rig. Rolling Stone ranked him the 75th-greatest guitarist of all time.

**Key Contributions to Tool:**
- Primary architect of riff foundations across all albums — some *Fear Inoculum* (2019) riffs traced back to ideas Jones wrote in the mid-1990s
- Directed the majority of Tool's music videos (stop-motion, clay, visual effects)
- Art directed album packaging; led Grammy-winning *10,000 Days* design with Alex Grey
- Introduced Alex Grey to the band's visual orbit in 1999

**Side Projects / Other Work:**
- No active solo project; Jones has consistently described his creative energy as entirely Tool-centric
- Continues to work as a visual artist and sculptor alongside the band

### 3. DANNY CAREY
**Role:** Drummer — Tool (1990–present)

**Full Name:** Daniel Edwin Carey
**Born:** May 10, 1961 — Lawrence, Kansas

**Bio:**
Daniel Edwin Carey grew up in Lawrence, Kansas, building a drumming foundation in jazz before expanding into progressive and experimental rock. He studied tabla with master percussionist Aloke Dutta, incorporating Indian classical rhythmic structures alongside Western jazz and rock techniques, and has pursued independent study in sacred geometry, metaphysics, and occult philosophy — all of which inform how he conceives of and builds rhythmic patterns. His approach to complex time signatures is notably intuitive: rather than counting bars, Carey focuses on establishing an "inner pulse" and playing to the emotional feel of each piece. Rolling Stone ranked him the 26th-greatest drummer of all time, and he is widely cited by peers and critics as among the greatest drummers alive. His performance on *Lateralus*' "Ticks & Leeches" placed third on NutSie.com's Top 100 Rock Drum Performances.

**Key Contributions to Tool:**
- Polyrhythmic and polymeric frameworks that define Tool's compositional complexity — most famously the Fibonacci-sequence time signatures of "Lateralus" (9/8, 8/8, 7/8 pattern)
- Sampling innovations: on *Lateralus*, sampled himself breathing through a tube to simulate Buddhist monk chanting on "Parabol"; banged piano strings for sample textures on "Reflection"
- On *Fear Inoculum*, proposed the concept of making the album "one long song" — an idea that shaped the final structure of multi-movement extended tracks
- Also contributed synthesizer work on *Fear Inoculum*

**Side Projects / Other Work:**
- **Volto!** — jazz fusion group
- **Pigmy Love Circus** — experimental rock collective
- **Legend of the Seagullmen** — rock project with various collaborators
- **Beat** — additional side project

### 4. JUSTIN CHANCELLOR
**Role:** Bassist — Tool (1995–present)

**Full Name:** Justin Gunnar Walter Chancellor
**Born:** November 19, 1971 — London, England

**Bio:**
Born in London of English and Norwegian descent, Justin Chancellor was educated at Tonbridge School in Kent and later studied at Durham University before music pulled him toward a professional path. He played bass in the British band Peach, which released the album *Giving Birth to a Stone* and two EPs; the band supported Tool on a European tour in 1994, which led directly to Chancellor's recruitment. When original bassist Paul D'Amour departed in 1995, Chancellor relocated to the United States and joined the band in time for the recording of *Ænima*, making his full debut on its 1996 release. He became known for his work with Wal bass guitars — particularly a MKII 4-string acquired during the *Ænima* sessions — and for his use of the DigiTech Bass Whammy to create harmonic layers and textural depth throughout the catalog. Off the musical stage, Chancellor co-owned the Topanga, California bookstore Lobal Orning (closed 2008) and has collaborated with artists including Death Grips, Primus, and Author & Punisher.

**Key Contributions to Tool:**
- Foundational bass voice on all four albums since *Ænima* (1996) through *Fear Inoculum* (2019)
- Signature use of Wal bass + DigiTech Bass Whammy creates harmonic overtone layers that blur the line between bass and texture
- Collaborative instrumental composition: co-developed the riff and rhythmic architecture of tracks alongside Jones and Carey in extended band jams
- Credited as co-writer on all Tool compositions since joining

**Side Projects / Other Work:**
- Collaborations with Death Grips, Primus, Author & Punisher
- Lobal Orning bookstore, Topanga, CA (co-owned, 2000s)

### 5. PAUL D'AMOUR
**Role:** Original Bassist — Tool (1990–1995)

**Full Name:** Paul D'Amour
**Born:** 1968/1969 — Spokane, Washington

**Bio:**
Paul D'Amour was introduced to the band through Adam Jones and became Tool's bassist despite being primarily a guitarist — a detail that would eventually shape his departure. Originally a guitarist who adapted to bass for the band's formation, D'Amour appeared on Tool's debut EP *Opiate* (1992) and the landmark debut album *Undertow* (1993), where his aggressive picked bass tone on a Chris Squire Signature Rickenbacker became a defining textural element of early Tool's sound. He is credited as co-writer on all *Undertow* tracks except "Bottom" (co-written with Henry Rollins). In late 1995, during early *Ænima* sessions, D'Amour left the band. According to Danny Carey, he departed because he "wanted to play guitar rather than bass." D'Amour would later join Ministry as bassist in 2019, appearing on *Moral Hygiene* (2021) and *Hopiumforthemasses* (2024).

**Key Contributions to Tool:**
- Foundational co-writer on all *Undertow* tracks — the album that introduced Tool to a mainstream audience
- His picked Rickenbacker bass tone defines the sonic character of *Opiate* and *Undertow*
- Central member during the band's formative Los Angeles years (1990–1995)

**Subsequent Projects:**
- **Lusk** (1995–1997): Psychedelic pop group, released *Free Mars*
- **Replicants**: Cover band with Ken Andrews and Greg Edwards
- **Feersum Ennjin** (2005–2011): Solo project
- **Lesser Key** (2013–present)
- **Ministry** (2019–present): Bassist on two albums

### 6. ALEX GREY
**Role:** Visionary Painter — Tool's primary visual collaborator (1999–present)

**Full Name:** Alexander Velzy Grey
**Born:** November 29, 1953

**Bio:**
Alex Grey is an American visionary artist whose paintings map the physical, psychic, and spiritual anatomy of the human form — rendering the body in transparent layers, like an X-ray revealing muscle, skeleton, nervous system, and luminous energy fields simultaneously. A spiritual experience in 1975 became the core of his artistic mission: "all opposites were connected by the different shades of grey," an insight that also inspired his chosen surname. His Chapel of Sacred Mirrors (CoSM), co-founded with his wife Allyson Grey in 1996, grew into a 40-acre retreat and gallery complex in Wappinger, New York, housing the Entheon: Sanctuary of Visionary Art. Adam Jones discovered Grey's work in 1999 and brought him into Tool's visual world; since then, Grey has created artwork for three consecutive Tool studio albums — *Lateralus* (2001), *10,000 Days* (2006), and *Fear Inoculum* (2019). The *10,000 Days* packaging, which Grey designed with Jones and which used stereoscopic 3D glasses to reveal depth in the imagery, won the 2006 Grammy Award for Best Recording Package. His 15-foot painting *Net of Being* — inspired by an ayahuasca journey — became one of the most iconic images associated with Tool's *Fear Inoculum* era.

**Key Contributions to Tool's Creative World:**
- Album artwork for *Lateralus*, *10,000 Days*, and *Fear Inoculum*
- Co-designer (with Jones) of the Grammy-winning *10,000 Days* stereoscopic packaging
- *Net of Being* became a signature visual for the *Fear Inoculum* campaign
- Philosophical alignment: Grey's synthesis of science, spirituality, and psychedelic experience mirrors Tool's own thematic territory

**Other Work:**
- Chapel of Sacred Mirrors (CoSM) / Entheon gallery complex, Wappinger, NY
- Ongoing painting practice; *Sacred Mirrors* series (Chapel namesake)
- Author of several books including *Sacred Mirrors*, *The Mission of Art*, and *Net of Being*

### A PERFECT CIRCLE
**Active:** 1999–present (hiatus periods)
**Core Members:** Maynard James Keenan (vocals), Billy Howerdel (guitar, primary composer)

**Origin:** Billy Howerdel had worked as a guitar tech and audio engineer for bands including Nine Inch Nails and Tool through the early 1990s, accumulating home studio demos over nearly a decade. He had originally envisioned Cocteau Twins singer Elizabeth Fraser as vocalist; when she was unavailable, Keenan — who had offered Howerdel a room in his North Hollywood home — heard the demos and said: *"I can hear myself singing [those songs]."* The band formed in 1999.

**Relationship to Tool:** Keenan has been explicit that A Perfect Circle is a fully separate entity, not a side project. He described Tool as more "left-brain masculine," with APC as "far more emotional and intuitive" and "right-brain feminine." The band signed to Virgin Records specifically so it would be treated as its own commercial entity, not a Tool offshoot.

**Key Albums:**
- *Mer de Noms* (2000): Debuted #4 Billboard 200, selling 188,000 copies first week — highest-selling debut by a rock band at that time
- *Thirteenth Step* (2003): #2 Billboard 200, 233,000 first-week copies
- *Emotive* (2004): Political covers album, #2 Billboard 200
- *Eat the Elephant* (2018): Return after 14-year album hiatus

### PUSCIFER
**Active:** 2003–present
**Core Members:** Maynard James Keenan (vocals), Mat Mitchell (guitar, bass, keys, programming), Carina Round (vocals, keys, percussion)

**Origin:** Named after a fictional band from the comedy series *Mr. Show*, Puscifer was conceived as Keenan's most open-ended creative vessel — *"simply a playground for the various voices in my head"* where his *"Id, Ego, and Anima all come together."* The project blends electronic, art rock, post-industrial, and theatrical performance art elements. Keenan has described Puscifer as functioning *"as much a clothing line as it is a band,"* reflecting its broad multimedia identity.

**Albums:**
- *"V" Is for Vagina* (2007)
- *Conditions of My Parole* (2011)
- *Money Shot* (2015)
- *Existential Reckoning* (2020)
- *Normal Isn't* (2026)
