## 1990

### Formation — Los Angeles, California
**Date:** 1990
**Event:** Tool forms in Los Angeles from a network of mutual connections
**Context:** Maynard James Keenan and guitarist Adam Jones met through a mutual friend in 1989; after Jones heard a recording of Keenan's previous band project, he convinced Keenan to form a new group. Drummer Danny Carey lived above Keenan and was introduced to Jones by Tom Morello (an old high school friend of Jones). Carey began playing with them partly, as he put it, because he "felt kinda sorry for them" when other invited musicians failed to show. Bassist Paul D'Amour completed the founding lineup, and the four began practicing and performing locally across the Los Angeles area.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Tool (band) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tool_(band)
> Verbatim: "Carey happened to live above Keenan and was introduced to Jones by Tom Morello, an old high school friend of Jones, and Carey began playing in their sessions because he 'felt kinda sorry for them.'"

## 1991

### 72826 Demo Tape — Independent Release
**Date:** December 21, 1991
**Event:** Tool self-releases limited cassette demo EP, *72826*
**Context:** Recorded between October and December 1991, approximately 1,500 cassettes were pressed and distributed locally. The EP included early versions of "Hush," "Part of Me," "Crawl Away," and "Sober," plus live recordings of "Cold and Ugly" and "Jerk-Off" captured at the Jello Loft on New Year's Eve 1991 — the band's second-ever public show. The catalog number 72826 corresponds to the word "SATAN" spelled out on a landline telephone keypad.
**Source:** Discogs — Tool – 72826 — https://www.discogs.com/release/1684003-Tool-72826; Tool Wiki (Fandom) — https://toolband.fandom.com/wiki/72826

### Signed to Zoo Entertainment
**Date:** November 1991
**Event:** Tool sign with Zoo Entertainment after only their seventh live gig
**Context:** After almost two years of local rehearsals and performances across Los Angeles, the band attracted major label interest and signed to Zoo Entertainment. The speed of the signing — after just seven shows — was exceptional and reflected the intensity of interest in the band's live performances.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Zoo Entertainment — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoo_Entertainment_(record_label); Louder Sound — "We tore them apart with chainsaws" — https://www.loudersound.com/features/tool-the-birth-of-the-band

## 1992

### *Opiate* EP — March 10, 1992
**Date:** March 10, 1992
**Event:** Zoo Entertainment releases Tool's debut EP, *Opiate*
**Context:** Six tracks across approximately 21 minutes, *Opiate* introduced Tool's dark, groove-driven sound to a wider audience. The EP includes "Hush," "Part of Me," and the visceral closer "Jerk-Off." Several tracks recorded during the *Opiate* sessions but not selected for the EP would later resurface on *Undertow*. The release set the stage for an aggressive touring campaign.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Opiate (EP) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opiate_(EP); Discogs — https://www.discogs.com/master/1061-Tool-Opiate
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "Opiate is an extended play by American rock band Tool, released on March 10, 1992, through Zoo Entertainment."

## 1993

### *Undertow* — April 6, 1993
**Date:** April 6, 1993
**Event:** Tool releases debut full-length album *Undertow* on Zoo Entertainment
**Context:** *Undertow* immediately distinguished Tool from the prevailing grunge scene with its locked-groove heaviness, precise rhythmic interplay, and Keenan's confrontational lyricism. The album reached platinum status quickly, fueled by relentless touring. Key tracks include "Prison Sex," "Sober," and "Crawl Away."
**Source:** Wikipedia — Undertow (Tool album) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Undertow_(Tool_album); WMMR — https://wmmr.com/listicle/tools-debut-album-undertow-turns-30-today/
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "Undertow is the debut studio album by American rock band Tool, released on April 6, 1993, by Zoo Entertainment."

### Lollapalooza 1993 — Second Stage to Main Stage
**Date:** Summer 1993
**Event:** Tool plays Lollapalooza; promoted from second stage to main stage mid-tour
**Context:** Tool initially earned a second-stage slot at the 1993 Lollapalooza festival but their crowd draw became impossible to ignore. Tour management, including co-founder Ted Gardner, moved them up to the main stage partway through the run. The exposure dramatically accelerated *Undertow*'s commercial trajectory, contributing to a gold certification from the RIAA in September 1993.
**Source:** Tool (band) — Wikipedia — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tool_(band); Past Daily — https://pastdaily.com/2019/11/30/tool-lollapalooza-des-moines-1993-past-daily-backstage-weekend/

## 1995

### Justin Chancellor Replaces Paul D'Amour
**Date:** 1995
**Event:** Founding bassist Paul D'Amour departs; Justin Chancellor joins Tool
**Context:** D'Amour left the band to pursue other musical interests. Tool auditioned numerous replacements — including Kyuss's Scott Reeder, Filter's Frank Cavanaugh, and Jane's Addiction's Eric Avery — before selecting Justin Chancellor, a member of British band Peach who had previously toured alongside Tool. Chancellor's fluid, melodic bass style would become a defining element of Tool's sound on every subsequent record.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Aenima — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%86nima; Loudwire — https://loudwire.com/tool-aenima-facts/
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "It is the first album by Tool to feature bassist Justin Chancellor, who replaced original bassist Paul D'Amour the year prior."

## 1996

### *Aenima* — September 17, 1996
**Date:** September 17, 1996
**Event:** Tool releases second studio album *Aenima* via Zoo/Volcano Entertainment
**Context:** *Aenima* represented a dramatic leap in compositional ambition — longer songs, more complex structures, and a deeper engagement with Jungian psychology and altered consciousness. The album is dedicated to comedian and philosopher Bill Hicks, who died in February 1994; an audio sample of Hicks appears on the closing track "Third Eye." The album's visual design includes imagery of California sinking into the ocean, referencing both Hicks's "Arizona Bay" material and the song "Aenema." *Aenima* debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 and has been certified double platinum in the United States.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Aenima — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%86nima; Albumism — https://albumism.com/features/tribute-celebrating-25-years-of-tool-aenima
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "The band dedicated the album to comedian Bill Hicks, who they felt was going in the same direction as them... An image of California before and after a major earthquake is shown in the tray behind where the disc lies — a reference to the song 'Aenema' and the Arizona Bay sketch by Bill Hicks."

### *Aenima* Tour Begins — Fall 1996
**Date:** Fall 1996 (Pomona, CA debut)
**Event:** Tool launches the *Aenima* tour, debuting new material live
**Context:** The tour opened in Pomona, California, where the band debuted "Forty-Six & 2," "Hooker with a Penis," "Jimmy," and "Third Eye" live for the first time. The tour extended through 1997, including a headline slot at Lollapalooza from June 25 to August 16, 1997, and a 41-date Ozzfest run across North America in Summer 1998.

## 2000

### *Salival* Box Set — December 2000
**Date:** December 2000
**Event:** Tool releases *Salival* — a limited-edition live/rarities box set in CD/VHS and CD/DVD formats
**Context:** Released to bridge the five-year gap between *Aenima* (1996) and the forthcoming *Lateralus*, *Salival* included live performances, a cover of "No Quarter" (Led Zeppelin) with an extended arrangement, and the studio track "L'Via L'Viaquez." It was issued in limited quantities and became a prized collector's item. The release signaled that new studio material was in development.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Salival — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salival; ProgArchives — https://www.progarchives.com/album.asp?id=6053

### A Perfect Circle — *Mer de Noms* — May 23, 2000
**Date:** May 23, 2000
**Event:** Maynard James Keenan releases APC debut album *Mer de Noms* with guitarist Billy Howerdel
**Context:** A Perfect Circle had been in development for roughly a decade — Howerdel had been accumulating riffs and demos while working as a guitar tech for major rock acts, storing gear at Keenan's house along the way. *Mer de Noms* debuted at number four on the Billboard 200, selling 188,000 copies in its first week — at the time the highest-charting debut for a rock album. Keenan's involvement reassured Tool fans that he remained artistically active during the long gap between Tool records.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Mer de Noms — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mer_de_Noms; A Perfect Circle — Wikipedia — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Perfect_Circle
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "Mer de Noms debuted at number four on the Billboard 200, selling 188,000 copies in its first week, making it the highest-selling debut album for a rock band."

## 2001

### *Lateralus* — May 15, 2001
**Date:** May 15, 2001
**Event:** Tool releases third studio album *Lateralus* on Volcano Records
**Context:** *Lateralus* is widely regarded as Tool's most technically and conceptually ambitious record. The title track's vocal syllable count directly mirrors the Fibonacci sequence (1-1-2-3-5-8-13), and the time signatures of the chorus shift through 9/8, 8/8, and 7/8 — the digits of 987, the 16th Fibonacci number. Visual collaborator Alex Grey, whose paintings grace the artwork, would become a long-term artistic partner. The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and has been certified platinum multiple times. The elaborate live production supporting the tour incorporated contortionist performers, Grey's tapestry art, and sophisticated projection work.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Lateralus — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateralus; Revolver — https://www.revolvermag.com/music/fighting-friction-fibonacci-tool-look-back-lateralus/
> Verbatim (Revolver): "The time signatures of the chorus change from 9/8 to 8/8 to 7/8, and 987 is the 16th number of the Fibonacci sequence."

### "Schism" Single — January 15, 2001
**Date:** January 15, 2001
**Event:** "Schism" released as lead single ahead of *Lateralus*
**Context:** "Schism" reached #1 on the Mainstream Rock chart and became one of Tool's signature songs, known for its asymmetric time signature shifts and the cryptic, relationship-fracturing lyric. The single won the Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance at the 44th Annual Grammy Awards ceremony on February 27, 2002 — Tool's first Grammy win.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Lateralus — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateralus; Blabbermouth — https://blabbermouth.net/news/tool-win-grammy-for-best-metal-performance
> Verbatim: "Tool win Grammy for 'Best Metal Performance' for 'Schism' at the 44th Annual Grammy Awards."

## 2002

### Grammy Award — Best Metal Performance for "Schism"
**Date:** February 27, 2002
**Event:** Tool wins their first Grammy at the 44th Annual Grammy Awards (Staples Center, Los Angeles)
**Context:** "Schism" took the Grammy for Best Metal Performance, validating Tool's broad appeal beyond the metal underground. The ceremony was held at the Staples Center in Los Angeles. The win also brought increased mainstream attention to *Lateralus* more than nine months after its release.
**Source:** Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance — Wikipedia — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Metal_Performance

### *Lateralus* Tour — Enhanced 2002 Stage Production
**Date:** Summer 2002
**Event:** The 2002 leg of the *Lateralus* tour adds major visual upgrades
**Context:** The 2002 summer leg introduced large tapestries by Alex Grey (creator of the *Lateralus* album art), giant floating balls referencing the "Parabola" music video, and sparkling confetti. The contortionist duo Osseus Labyrint, who appeared in the "Schism" video, performed live during shows. The expanded production underscored Tool's philosophy of the concert as an immersive multi-sensory event, not merely a performance.

## 2003

### A Perfect Circle — *Thirteenth Step* — September 16, 2003
**Date:** September 16, 2003
**Event:** APC releases second album *Thirteenth Step*, debuting at number 2 on the Billboard 200
**Context:** *Thirteenth Step* is a concept album examining addiction and recovery — its title references the 12-step programs of Alcoholics Anonymous, with the "thirteenth step" implying what comes after or fails within that structure. The album sold over 231,000 copies in its first week and remained on the charts for 78 weeks. Keenan's continued work with APC filled what would become an unusually long studio gap for Tool.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Thirteenth Step — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirteenth_Step; Badger Herald — https://badgerherald.com/artsetc/2003/09/25/the-13th-step-to-rec/

## 2006

### *10,000 Days* — May 2, 2006 (North America)
**Date:** May 2, 2006 (North America); April 28, 2006 (Europe)
**Event:** Tool releases fourth studio album *10,000 Days* on Volcano Records
**Context:** The album's emotional centerpiece is the two-part suite "Wings for Marie (Pt 1)" and "10,000 Days (Wings Pt 2)," a tribute to Keenan's mother Judith Marie Gridley, who was paralyzed by a stroke and spent approximately 10,000 days (roughly 27 years) in that condition before her death. The album sold 564,000 copies in its first week and debuted at number one on the Billboard 200. Its packaging — a stereoscopic viewer embedded in the gatefold — won the Grammy Award for Best Recording Package at the 49th Annual Grammy Awards in 2007.
**Source:** Wikipedia — 10,000 Days — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/10,000_Days_(Tool_album); Ultimate Classic Rock — https://ultimateclassicrock.com/tool-10000-days/
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "The title 10,000 Days additionally refers to the amount of time that Maynard's mother, Judith Marie Gridley, was paralyzed."

### *10,000 Days* Tour — 2006–2007
**Date:** April 30, 2006 (Coachella) through December 1, 2007
**Event:** Tool tours extensively behind *10,000 Days* — 213 concerts across two years
**Context:** The tour opened at Coachella on April 30, 2006 with supporting acts Isis and Mastodon. Tool headlined Download Festival at Castle Donington, UK on the opening day of the 2006 edition. A headline appearance at Bonnaroo in June 2007 featured a guest appearance from Tom Morello (Rage Against the Machine) during "Lateralus." The tour ran 213 shows total before wrapping on December 1, 2007.

## 2007

### Puscifer — *"V" Is for Vagina* — October 30, 2007
**Date:** October 30, 2007
**Event:** Maynard James Keenan launches Puscifer with debut album *"V" Is for Vagina*
**Context:** Puscifer began as Keenan's experimental, comedic side project — a creative subconscious outlet for material that did not fit Tool or APC. The debut album was largely assembled in tour buses, hotel rooms, and studios scattered across the country while Keenan was on the road with Tool. The sound drew on electronic production, Motown-inflected grooves, and dark humor. The album debuted at number 25 on the Billboard 200, selling approximately 27,000 copies in its first week.
**Source:** Wikipedia — Puscifer — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puscifer; Louder Sound — https://www.loudersound.com/features/puscifer-story-behind-the-first-album
> Verbatim (Wikipedia): "Created in a tour bus, in several hotel rooms, and in various studios around the country while Keenan toured with Tool, the album is a radical departure from Keenan's contributions in Tool and A Perfect Circle."

## 2019

### Full Catalog Arrives on Streaming — August 2, 2019
**Date:** August 2, 2019
**Event:** Tool's entire back catalog goes live on Spotify, Apple Music, and Tidal
**Context:** Tool had been one of the most prominent and longest-standing holdouts against digital streaming — for most of the streaming era, their albums were unavailable on any platform. The catalog drop (covering *Opiate*, *Undertow*, *Aenima*, *Lateralus*, and *10,000 Days*) landed just weeks before *Fear Inoculum*'s release. Keenan announced it with characteristic wit, writing that "Our obsession with, and dream of, a world where BetaMax and Laser Disc rule has ended."
**Source:** Spin — https://www.spin.com/2019/08/tool-music-listen-streaming-spotify-apple-music-listen/; Engadget — https://www.engadget.com/2019-07-29-tool-catalog-streaming-services-new-album.html
> Verbatim (Maynard James Keenan, via Engadget): "Our obsession with, and dream of, a world where BetaMax and Laser Disc rule has ended. Time for us to move on."

### *Fear Inoculum* — August 30, 2019
**Date:** August 30, 2019
**Event:** Tool releases fifth studio album *Fear Inoculum* — their first album in 13 years
**Context:** The 13-year wait between *10,000 Days* (2006) and *Fear Inoculum* was one of the longest gaps between studio albums in the history of major-label rock. The album consists of seven tracks with an average runtime exceeding 10 minutes each; the title track alone runs 10 minutes 21 seconds. It debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 with 270,000 album-equivalent units (248,000 pure sales) — the biggest opening week for any rock album in over a year, dislodging Taylor Swift's *Lover* from the top spot. Two tracks received Grammy nominations: "Fear Inoculum" (Best Rock Song) and "7empest" (Best Metal Performance, which it won).
**Source:** Wikipedia — Fear Inoculum — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear_Inoculum; Billboard — https://www.billboard.com/pro/tools-fear-inoculum-debuts-at-no-1-on-billboard-200-chart-rock-album/; CNBC — https://www.cnbc.com/2019/09/09/tools-fear-inoculum-dethrones-taylor-swift-on-billboard-200-chart.html
> Verbatim (Billboard): "Tool's 'Fear Inoculum' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Chart With Biggest Week For a Rock Album in Over 1 Year."

## 2020

### *Fear Inoculum* Tour Suspended — COVID-19
**Date:** March 11–12, 2020
**Event:** Tool's touring halts abruptly as COVID-19 closes venues nationwide
**Context:** Tool performed through a March 11, 2020 show in Portland, Oregon. The following night's show at Matthew Knight Arena in Eugene, Oregon was canceled as nationwide lockdowns took effect. The band later officially canceled all postponed spring 2020 dates outright rather than rescheduling indefinitely, which was noted as a gesture of goodwill toward fans during economic hardship. The Eugene date would become a symbolic marker — Tool pledged to resume there when touring eventually restarted.

## 2025

### "Tool Live in the Sand" — First South American and Caribbean Dates
**Date:** March–April 2025
**Event:** Tool's debut South American and Caribbean tour, plus three Lollapalooza headlining slots
**Context:** Tool announced their first-ever South American dates under the banner "Tool Live in the Sand," and their debut Caribbean outing. Additional Mexican dates were added (Monterrey March 12, Guadalajara March 18).
