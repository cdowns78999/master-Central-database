## CATEGORY 6 — Music Publishing

### CAT-6-TILE-1 | Music Publishing | FACT
**Claim:** Publishing royalties split 50/50 between the writer's share (always goes to the songwriter directly via their PRO) and the publisher's share (goes to the publisher, who pays the songwriter per their deal).
**Source 1:** [Songwriting Royalties Explained: Writer's Share vs Publisher's Share](https://blog.songtrust.com/songwriting-royalties-explained-writers-vs-publishers-share) — Blog
**Source 2:** [What is the Difference Between the Writer's Share and Publisher's Share?](https://support.tunecore.com/hc/en-us/articles/115006689148-What-is-the-Difference-Between-the-Writer-s-Share-and-Publisher-s-Share) — Blog
---

### CAT-6-TILE-2 | Music Publishing | FACT
**Claim:** Admin publishing deals: songwriter retains 100% copyright ownership. Publisher administers collection globally and takes 10-25% commission during the deal term only.
**Source 1:** [The Three Types of Music Publishing Agreements, And Why They're Important](https://lawyerdrummer.com/2014/04/music-publishing-agreements/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Publishing Deal vs Admin Deal: Which Do You Need?](https://orphiq.com/resources/publishing-deal-vs-admin-deal) — Blog
---

### CAT-6-TILE-3 | Music Publishing | FACT
**Claim:** Co-publishing deals: songwriter assigns 50% of their copyright to the publisher. Publisher collects its percentage from all songs created during the deal term -- often for the life of the copyright (70+ years after death). In exchange, artist typically receives advances, creative services, and sync pitching.
**Source 1:** [What's the Deal: Understanding Co-Publishing & Admin Deals](https://www.ascap.com/help/career-development/whats-the-deal-michael-eames-pen-music) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Music Publishing Deals Available to Songwriters and Creators](https://blog.songtrust.com/music-publishing-deals) — Blog
---

### CAT-6-TILE-4 | Music Publishing | FACT
**Claim:** Full publishing deals: publisher owns 100% of the copyright. Songwriter typically receives a larger advance. Songwriter still receives writer's share royalties through their PRO. These deals are increasingly rare and generally unfavorable for artists.
**Source 1:** [4 Main Types of Publishing Contracts](https://blog.symphonic.com/2022/09/22/types-of-publishing-contracts/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [What Are the Three Basic Types of Music Publishing Deals for Songwriters?](https://www.romanolaw.com/what-are-the-three-basic-types-of-music-publishing-deals-for-songwriters/) — Blog
---

### CAT-6-TILE-5 | Music Publishing | FACT
**Claim:** Global collection: publishers register songs through sub-publishers in every major territory. Without global registration, royalties generated in foreign markets (UK, Germany, Japan, etc.) sit uncollected. Sub-publisher fees typically range 15-20% of the foreign income collected.
**Source 1:** [How Global Royalty Collection Really Works](https://www.songtrust.com/how-global-royalty-collection-works) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Collect Global Music Royalties | Worldwide Music Income](https://zachbornheimermusic.com/music-business/global-royalties-how-to-collect-internationally/) — Blog
---

### CAT-6-TILE-6 | Music Publishing | PRACTICE
**Claim:** For independent artists who write their own songs, the minimum viable publishing setup is: (1) register with a PRO (ASCAP or BMI in the US), (2) sign up with a publishing administrator (Songtrust, TuneCore Publishing, DistroKid Songwriting add-on) to handle global collection and MLC registration, and (3) register every released song with your PRO and publishing admin before or immediately upon release.
**Source 1:** [Songwriters! Registering With ASCAP or BMI Is Not Enough To Get Paid](https://aristake.com/songwriter-royalties/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [How to Register Your Music for Royalties: ASCAP vs BMI Guide](https://artistrack.com/register-music-royalties-ascap-bmi-guide/) — Blog
---

### CAT-6-TILE-7 | Music Publishing | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Skipping PRO registration -- performance royalties from radio, streaming, and public performance accumulate from day one but are only payable to registered writers.
**Source 1:** [How to Register Songs with ASCAP/BMI: A Step-by-Step Guide for Songwriters](https://www.delgadoentertainmentlaw.com/post/how-to-register-songs-with-ascap-bmi-a-step-by-step-guide-for-songwriters) — Blog
**Source 2:** [BMI ASCAP: My Essential 2026 Guide to Music Royalties](https://playhousesound.com/step-by-step-guide-to-registering-with-bmi-or-ascap/) — Blog

### CAT-6-TILE-8 | Music Publishing | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Signing a full publishing deal without understanding that the publisher may own the copyright for 70+ years after your death -- this is an irreversible decision without a strong reversion clause.
**Source 1:** [Navigating US Copyright Termination Rights](https://www.wipo.int/en/web/wipo-magazine/articles/navigating-us-copyright-termination-rights-38190) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Reversion Clause (Songtrust Glossary)](https://www.songtrust.com/music-publishing-glossary/glossary-reversion-clause) — Blog

### CAT-6-TILE-9 | Music Publishing | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Not distinguishing between a publishing admin and a full publisher -- an admin collects royalties; a publisher also pitches songs, invests in development, and takes ownership.
**Source 1:** [Music Publishing Administration 101](https://blog.songtrust.com/music-publishing-administration-101) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Understanding Music Rights: Publishing Administrators, Publishers, Copyright, and Neighboring Rights](https://support.symdistro.com/hc/en-us/articles/203680905-Understanding-Music-Rights-Publishing-Administrators-Publishers-Copyright-and-Neighboring-Rights) — Blog

### CAT-6-TILE-10 | Music Publishing | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Not including co-writers in PRO registration -- if you co-wrote a song, all writers must be registered with their respective PROs or royalties for their share won't flow.
**Source 1:** [What Co-Writers Need to Know About Songwriting Splits](https://www.ascap.com/help/registering-your-music/Splitsville) — Blog

### CAT-6-TILE-11 | Music Publishing | MISTAKE

## CATEGORY 7 — Record Labels (Major vs Indie)

### CAT-7-TILE-1 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | FACT
**Claim:** Traditional recording deal: label owns the master recording, advances recording costs against future royalties, and pays the artist 10-20% of revenue as a royalty rate. The artist recoups the advance before seeing any royalty income.
**Source 1:** [Record Deals and Music Contracts Explained](https://orphiq.com/resources/record-deals-explained) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Understanding Label Advances and Recoupment: What Recording Artists Actually Owe](https://blog.promise.legal/startup-central/understanding-label-advances-and-recoupment-what-recording-artists-actually-owe/) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-2 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | FACT
**Claim:** 360 deal (multiple rights deal): label takes a percentage (typically 15-25%) of all artist revenue streams -- touring, merch, brand deals, publishing -- in addition to recording royalties. Became standard at major labels in the 2010s as album revenue declined.
**Source 1:** [What is a 360 Deal in Music Contracts?](https://blog.discmakers.com/2024/09/what-is-a-360-deal/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Ultimate Guide to 360 Deals in the Music Industry](https://www.corderolawgroup.com/blog/2025/360-deals-music-industry) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-3 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | FACT
**Claim:** Licensing deal: artist retains master ownership and licenses the recording to the label for a fixed term (e.g., 5-7 years). At the end of the term, masters revert to the artist. Increasingly common for established independent artists negotiating from a position of leverage.
**Source 1:** [Owning Your Master Rights | Retaining Music Master Recording Rights](https://blog.discmakers.com/2013/04/retaining-your-master-rights-smart-business/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Recording Contracts in Music Law: What Artists Need to Know](https://artandmedialaw.com/recording-contracts-music-law/) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-4 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | FACT
**Claim:** Joint venture (JV) deal: label funds all recording and marketing costs, keeps a running ledger of expenses, and after recoupment, profits are split 50/50 with the artist. More equitable structure but requires the label to believe in significant commercial potential.
**Source 1:** [Lesson 5: Profit Share Deals](https://www.curveroyaltysystems.com/royalties-101-recorded-music/lesson-5-profit-share-deals) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Recoupment in Record Deals: What Every Artist Should Know](https://www.juliaholtlaw.com/blog/understanding-recoupment-in-record-deals-a-guide-for-artists) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-5 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | FACT
**Claim:** Major label royalty range: 10-20% of revenue. Indie label royalty range: typically 50-70% of net revenue. What major labels provide that independents typically can't: $500K+ marketing budgets, terrestrial radio promotion infrastructure, international distribution and promotional staff.
**Source 1:** [Major vs Independent Labels: What's the Difference?](https://orphiq.com/resources/major-vs-independent-labels) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Indie vs. Major Record Labels: Which Is Right for You?](https://www.iconcollective.edu/indie-vs-major-record-labels) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-6 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | PRACTICE
**Claim:** In 2025, the strategic calculus of signing to a label vs. staying independent has shifted significantly. Artists with proven streaming traction, social media audiences, and touring revenue increasingly negotiate from a position of leverage, securing licensing deals rather than traditional ownership deals.
**Source 1:** [Independent vs Signed in 2025: What's Best for Your Music Career?](https://thebuzznetwork.net/blog/independent-vs-signed-in-2025-whats-best-for-your-music-career) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Artist Development Tactics for Record Labels in 2025](https://www.reprtoir.com/blog/artist-development-2025) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-7 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Signing a 360 deal that captures touring and merch income -- these revenue streams are the only guaranteed money for most artists, and giving away 15-25% of them to a label is often financially devastating.
**Source 1:** [What Is a 360 Deal? Artist and Label Split Explained](https://viewmaniac.com/360-deal/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [What is a 360 Deal? Music Industry Definition](https://soundcharts.com/en/blog/what-is-360-deal) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-8 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Not understanding recoupment -- labels recoup their advance AND recording costs AND sometimes marketing costs before the artist sees any royalty income. An artist can go platinum and still be 'unrecouped' on paper.
**Source 1:** [Recoupment in Record Deals: What Every Artist Should Know About Royalties, Contracts & Earnings](https://www.juliaholtlaw.com/blog/understanding-recoupment-in-record-deals-a-guide-for-artists) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Music Royalties Explained: The Ultimate Guide for 2026](https://www.indiemusicacademy.com/blog/music-royalties-explained) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-9 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Conflating 'advances' with 'salary' -- label advances are loans against future royalties. Every dollar of the advance must be earned back before royalties are paid, which can take years for most artists.
**Source 1:** [The Ultimate Guide To Record Label Contracts](https://playlistpush.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-record-label-contracts/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Music and Money: Recording Artist Royalties](https://www.ascap.com/help/music-business-101/money-recording) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-10 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Ignoring the option clause -- most label deals include options giving the label the right to sign subsequent albums at their discretion, potentially locking an artist in for 6-8 albums over a decade.
**Source 1:** [Understanding Record Deal Clauses: A Comprehensive Guide for Artists](https://www.rexiusrecords.com/understanding-record-deal-clauses-a-comprehensive-guide-for-artists/) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Options - How Recording Contracts Work](https://entertainment.howstuffworks.com/recording-contract6.htm) — Blog
---

### CAT-7-TILE-11 | Record Labels (Major vs Indie) | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Not negotiating a reversion clause -- if the label doesn't promote or release an album within a set timeframe, the rights should revert to the artist. Without this, masters can sit unused at a label indefinitely.
**Source 1:** [Catalog Reversion Rights](https://amworldgroup.com/glossary/entertainment/catalog-reversion-rights) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Master Ownership Clauses: Checklist for Artists](https://onlinemusiccontracts.com/master-ownership-clauses-checklist-for-artists/) — Blog

## CATEGORY 8 — Artist Management

### CAT-8-TILE-1 | Artist Management | FACT
**Claim:** Standard commission rate: 15-20% of gross income. Established managers with strong track records may command 20-25%. Some managers work on net income (after deducted expenses), which is significantly more favorable for the artist.
**Source 1:** [How Much Money Do Artist Managers Make?](https://smartistu.com/how-much-money-do-artist-managers-make) — Blog
**Source 2:** [What Artists' Managers Really Earn](https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/what-artists-managers-really-earn-its-not-cheap-to-be-available-24-7-6605758/) — Blog
---

### CAT-8-TILE-2 | Artist Management | FACT
**Claim:** Commissionable income typically includes: recording royalties, streaming income, touring and live show revenue, merchandise profits, endorsements and brand deals, publishing advances and royalties, and sync licensing fees.
**Source 1:** [Artist Management Contracts Your Ultimate Guide](https://www.corderolawgroup.com/blog/2025/artist-management-contracts) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Understanding Artist Management Agreements, Part 3: How Are Managers Paid, and How Much?](https://blog.sonicbids.com/understanding-artist-management-agreements-part-3-how-are-managers-paid-and-how-much) — Blog
---

### CAT-8-TILE-3 | Artist Management | FACT
**Claim:** Management contract term: typically 1-3 years with options for renewal. The initial term is a trial period -- both parties are evaluating fit. Deals rarely exceed 3 years on the first agreement.
**Source 1:** [5 Must-Have Clauses in Artist Management Contracts](https://blog.creativeintell.com/5-must-have-clauses-in-artist-management-contracts) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Key Contract Terms for Musicians and Managers](https://www.romanolaw.com/key-contract-terms-for-musicians-and-managers/) — Blog

### CAT-8-TILE-4 | Artist Management | FACT
**Claim:** Sunset clause: a standard contractual provision that gradually reduces the manager's commission percentage on deals made during their tenure after the contract ends -- typically declining to zero over 3-5 years post-term. This protects artists from paying full commission indefinitely on deals the former manager secured.
**Source 1:** [Sunset Clauses in the Music Industry](https://businesslawyergrandrapids.com/blog/sunset-clauses-in-the-music-industry) — Blog
**Source 2:** [The Sunset Clause in Management Agreements](https://medium.com/@1111MusicConsulting/the-sunset-clause-in-management-agreements-fde08f2ff674) — Blog
---

### CAT-8-TILE-5 | Artist Management | FACT
**Claim:** The manager's role: career strategy, contract negotiation, team building (booking agent, publicist, attorney), opportunity evaluation, label and publisher relationship management, budget oversight, and day-to-day problem solving. Managers do not book shows (that's a booking agent's job) or file legal documents (attorney's job).
**Source 1:** [What does a manager do for an artist? Key roles explained](https://www.artist.tools/post/what-does-a-manager-do-for-an-artist-key-roles-explained) — Blog
**Source 2:** [Artist Management 101: The Role of Music Talent Managers](https://soundcharts.com/en/blog/the-mechanics-of-management) — Blog
---

### CAT-8-TILE-6 | Artist Management | PRACTICE
**Claim:** Most successful management relationships begin informally -- a trusted person from the artist's existing network (friend, family member, fellow musician) starts managing before either party formalizes the arrangement. This organic start is fine in the early stages but must be formalized in writing before significant deals are being negotiated.
**Source 1:** [Artist Management Contract Template and Guide](https://www.artist.tools/post/artist-management-contract-template-and-guide) — Blog
**Source 2:** [How to Manage an Artist in 2025: The Complete Guide](https://stagent.com/blog/how-to-manage-an-artist) — Blog

### CAT-8-TILE-7 | Artist Management | MISTAKE
**Claim:** Signing a management deal too early -- before you have consistent income, a body of work, and clear career direction, giving up 15-20% of gross income to a manager provides limited ROI.
**Source 1:** [6 Red Flags to Watch for before Signing with a Manager, Agent, or A&R Rep](https://online.berklee.edu/takenote/6-red-flags-to-watch-for-before-signing-with-a-manager-agent-or-ar-rep/) — Blog

## CATEGORY 9 — Artist Branding

### CAT-9-TILE-1 | Artist Branding | FACT
**Claim:** Parasocial relationships: fans develop deep one-sided emotional bonds with artists through consistent storytelling, emotional disclosure, and perceived intimacy. Academic studies confirm these bonds activate the same neurological systems as real friendships, driving loyalty behaviors like repeat streaming, concert attendance, and merchandise purchase.

### CAT-9-TILE-2 | Artist Branding | FACT
**Claim:** Visual consistency is the fastest trust signal -- humans process images 60,000x faster than text, and consistent visual identity (color palette, typography, aesthetic) allows fans to instantly recognize an artist's content in a crowded feed without reading any text.
