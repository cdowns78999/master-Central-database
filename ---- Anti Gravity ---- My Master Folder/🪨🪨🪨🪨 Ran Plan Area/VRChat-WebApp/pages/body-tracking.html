Troubleshooting

Trackers drifting or losing position ▼

IMU-based trackers (SlimeVR, HaritoraX) drift over time. Recalibrate every 15–20 minutes by standing in a T-pose and running the reset command. Magnetic interference from speakers, metal desks, or wireless chargers can worsen drift. Move away from interference sources and enable yaw correction in your tracker software.

SteamVR not detecting trackers ▼

For Vive/Tundra trackers: ensure base stations have clear line of sight. Check that SteamVR's Bluetooth driver is not conflicting. Update firmware via SteamVR → Devices → Firmware. For SlimeVR: verify the SlimeVR server is running and trackers are connected via Wi-Fi (check IP assignments in router settings).

Avatar legs/feet clipping through floor ▼

This usually means your avatar's height calibration is off. In VRChat, go to Settings → Tracking & IK → Calibrate FBT. Stand straight, arms at sides, and press both triggers. If feet still clip, check that your hip tracker height is accurate and your play space floor level is calibrated in SteamVR.

FBT Enhances Avatar Embodiment

Research shows that full body tracking significantly increases the sense of avatar embodiment — the psychological feeling that your virtual body is your own. Users with FBT report stronger presence, more natural social interactions, and deeper emotional connection to their avatar identity.

Source: Aalborg University (AAU) Virtual Embodiment Studies
