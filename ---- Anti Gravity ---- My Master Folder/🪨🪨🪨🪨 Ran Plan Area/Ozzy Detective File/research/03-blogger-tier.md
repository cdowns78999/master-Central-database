# 03 — Blogger / Fan / Enthusiast-Tier Perspectives on Ozzy Osbourne

## Intro

This layer is where Ozzy's legacy breathes outside the press-release bubble. The "deep fans" tier lives on Metal Archives, Metal Injection, MetalSucks, Loudwire, Reddit threads, Substack diaries, and personal tribute blogs. These voices care less about chart position and more about which Jake E. Lee deep cut made them pick up a guitar, whose pinch harmonic defined their adolescence, and what Ozzy actually meant on the day he died. The following ten findings surface the community's rankings, debates, lore, and grief — the stuff critics tend to miss.

---

## Top Findings

### 1. Metal Injection — "10 Extremely Underrated Ozzy Osbourne Songs"
**Community/Outlet:** Metal Injection (core metal-news blog, long-running)
**Summary:** A deep-cut evangelism piece the fan community routinely cites when defending non-hit Ozzy material. It champions tracks like "Secret Loser" (the Jake E. Lee vocal-guitar interplay), "S.I.N." (the Zakk shred fans say gets overshadowed by "No More Tears"), "Bloodbath in Paradise" (Manson-themed, pinch-harmonic showcase), "Perry Mason" (an "underrated chorus" barely played live), and "Old LA Tonight" (ballad where Ozzy's voice sits front-and-center). The piece represents the enthusiast consensus that Ozzy's "best songs" list is far wider than the radio rotation implies.
**URL:** https://metalinjection.net/lists/10-extremely-underrated-ozzy-osbourne-songs

### 2. The Discourse Zone (Substack) — "10 Ozzy Songs That Got Me Through Life" by Tomas Maldonado
**Community/Outlet:** Independent fan Substack (personal-essay tier)
**Summary:** One of the best examples of the "lived-and-breathed" perspective. Maldonado narrates how individual Ozzy tracks attached to life events — "Paranoid" during Long COVID in 2020, "War Pigs" reshaping his politics on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, "Changes" shared with his stepson as a lesson that "music transcends color," "Diary of a Madman" reframed from demonic to mental-illness metaphor. He puts "I Don't Know" at #1 for its embrace of intellectual humility. This is exactly the kind of non-critic testimony that defines the fan tier.
**URL:** https://thediscoursezone.substack.com/p/10-ozzy-songs-that-got-me-through

### 3. Dark Forces Swing (Substack) — "Ozzy Osbourne, RIP (End of an Era)" by Hank Shteamer
**Community/Outlet:** Music-critic Substack with strong metal-fan readership (Shteamer is a respected veteran metal writer)
**Summary:** Captures the community's most honest framing of Ozzy post-mortem: "Ozzy's legacy is a complicated one, given that, depending on when you were born, you may have one of three or four or five different conceptions of who he is as an artist and pop-cultural figure." Fans in the comments debate the Sabbath-vs-solo-vs-reality-TV-vs-late-career Ozzys. This piece is cited repeatedly in other Substack tributes.
**URL:** https://darkforcesswing.substack.com/p/ozzy-osbourne-rip-end-of-an-era

### 4. Running Probably (Substack) — "A Tribute to Ozzy" by Paul Flannery
**Community/Outlet:** Fan-writer Substack
**Summary:** The emotional-core fan take. Flannery's thesis: "Ozzy never lied to us... time after time, through every media frenzy, he told us the truth." He frames Ozzy as the outsider, the fuck-up, the idiot, the fool — "Ozzy embodied them all." This authenticity-as-legacy argument is the single most repeated theme across fan tributes after his July 22, 2025 death.
**URL:** https://paulflannery.substack.com/p/a-tribute-to-ozzy

### 5. The Heavy Metal Citadel — "A Personal Tribute to the Prince of Darkness"
**Community/Outlet:** Independent metal blog
**Summary:** Written by a fan who never got to see Ozzy live. Centers the Birmingham farewell ("Back to the Beginning," July 5, 2025) as a full-circle moment — Metallica, Slayer, and Guns N' Roses joining for the send-off, Ozzy dying 17 days later. Grief balanced against gratitude. Highlights the *No More Tears* title track as a song that "still gives me chills" and calls out Randy Rhoads as the virtuoso who pulled Ozzy out of his post-Sabbath alcoholism spiral.
**URL:** https://www.heavymetalcitadel.com/newsreviews/ozzy-osbourne-tribute

### 6. Ultimate Classic Rock — "Ozzy Osbourne Guitarists: Ranked" (community-reflecting list)
**Community/Outlet:** Classic-rock/metal blog that mirrors fan consensus well
**Summary:** The definitive community ranking of Ozzy's guitar chair. Order: (1) Randy Rhoads — "the most pivotal musician" Ozzy ever worked with; (2) Zakk Wylde — the "heir apparent" who became the definitive sideman by longevity; (3) Jake E. Lee — the fretboard-fireworks guy who wrote half of *Bark at the Moon* and *The Ultimate Sin*; (4) Joe Holmes; (5) Brad Gillis; (6) Gus G; (7) Bernie Tormé; (8) Jerry Cantrell (covers-album fill-in); (9) Alex Skolnick (one UK gig); (10) Steve Vai (nearly-was). Matches fan-forum consensus on Steve Hoffman, Ultimate Guitar, and r/Metal threads.
**URL:** https://ultimateclassicrock.com/ozzy-guitarists-ranked/

### 7. Ultimate Guitar — "Jake E. Lee vs. Zakk Wylde: How One Guitarist Was Written Out of Ozzy's Legacy"
**Community/Outlet:** Ultimate Guitar (largest guitarist community on the web)
**Summary:** A recurring enthusiast grievance: Jake E. Lee gets short-changed in Ozzy retrospectives despite writing the backbone of two commercially successful albums. The piece argues Jake's era (1983–1987) was erased by Zakk's multi-decade dominance and by contractual/credit disputes. This article is frequently linked in r/Metal threads whenever Ozzy's guitarists come up — it has become the fan-consensus "Justice for Jake" essay.
**URL:** https://www.ultimate-guitar.com/articles/features/jake-e-lee-vs-zakk-wylde-how-one-guitarist-was-written-out-of-ozzys-legacy-185987

### 8. Encyclopaedia Metallum (Metal Archives) — Ozzy Osbourne Solo Discography
**Community/Outlet:** Metal Archives — the single most authoritative metalhead-run database on the internet
**Summary:** The fan-tier equivalent of Wikipedia for metal. Hosts community reviews and detailed credits for every Ozzy solo album (*Blizzard of Ozz* through *Ordinary Man*), plus live records (*Tribute*, *Live & Loud*, *Just Say Ozzy*) and compilations (*The Ultimate Ozzy*). Notably, *Blizzard of Ozz* is listed with the original 1980 UK release date (September 20) vs. 1981 US (March 27) — a detail fans argue about constantly. Per-album review scores exist on the site but could not be scraped here (403).
**URL:** https://www.metal-archives.com/bands/Ozzy_Osbourne/195

### 9. Loudwire / Metal Injection — The Bat-Biting Lore (Des Moines, Jan 20, 1982)
**Community/Outlet:** Loudwire + Metal Injection (the two loudest voices in metal blog-tier journalism)
**Summary:** The single most-passed-around piece of Ozzy lore. Consolidated fan consensus: Veterans Memorial Auditorium, Des Moines, Iowa, January 20, 1982, *Diary of a Madman* tour. A 17-year-old named Mark Neal threw the bat; he self-identified to the Des Moines Register that same year. The disputed detail fans still argue about: *was the bat alive or dead?* Rolling Stone (2004) said alive; Neal insists it had been dead for weeks, sealed in a baggie. Ozzy himself said he felt it twitch. Fans also note the story is routinely confused with the separate dove-biting incident at a CBS Records meeting in 1981.
**URLs:**
- https://loudwire.com/ozzy-osbourne-bat-incident-story-you-didnt-know/
- https://loudwire.com/ozzy-osbourne-bat-bite-story-oral-history/
- https://metalinjection.net/rock-metal-history/why-did-ozzy-osbourne-bite-the-head-off-a-bat-in-1981

### 10. "Back to the Beginning" — Villa Park, July 5, 2025 (fan-tier coverage)
**Community/Outlet:** Cross-source fan community (Rock Cellar, MetalSucks, Consequence, social)
**Summary:** The emotional hinge of the entire fan-tier narrative. Ozzy performed five songs from a black throne: "I Don't Know," "Mr. Crowley," "Suicide Solution," "Mama, I'm Coming Home," "Crazy Train." First reunion of the classic Sabbath lineup (Ozzy/Iommi/Butler/Ward) since 2005. Fan reports from Villa Park: "70,000 people started balling" during "Mama, I'm Coming Home." Ozzy died 17 days later (July 22, 2025). MetalSucks responded by reprogramming its playlist to 30 tracks (15 Ozzy solo, 15 Sabbath). "Crazy Train" cracked the Billboard Hot 100 on streaming surge post-death. Metal Hammer's Issue 404 is the official fan-press tribute issue.

## Fan Consensus Snapshots (cross-source)

- **Album tier list (solo):** *Blizzard of Ozz* and *Diary of a Madman* tied at the top, *No More Tears* the undisputed third, *Bark at the Moon* the Jake-era standout, *Ozzmosis* divides the room, everything after that is considered a fall-off by most fan rankings (Loudwire, Louder Sound, Consequence, Rate Your Music, Steve Hoffman Forums all converge here).
- **Guitarist debate:** Randy is the martyr-genius, Zakk is the definitive sound by tenure, Jake is the "written-out-of-history" fan-favorite underdog. The Jake-defender faction is louder on Ultimate Guitar and r/Metal than the mainstream press reflects.
- **Death reaction (July 22, 2025):** The community's pain was channeled through the Sabbath-family response — Iommi's "lost our brother" post, Geezer Butler's long-form essay (Deadline), Metallica's broken-heart post, and a massive streaming surge that put "Crazy Train" back on the Hot 100.
- **Lore fans pass around:** the bat (Des Moines, 1982), the dove (CBS Records, 1981 — *separate* incident), the Alamo urination arrest (1982), the ants, and the Diary-era Randy Rhoads plane crash (March 19, 1982). Fans routinely correct first-time listeners who conflate these.
