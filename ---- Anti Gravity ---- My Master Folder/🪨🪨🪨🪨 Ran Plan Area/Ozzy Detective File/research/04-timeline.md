# Ozzy Osbourne — Year-by-Year Timeline (1948–2025)

Compiled from publicly available sources. Format: `YEAR | headline | detail | source URL`. Disputed facts flagged inline.

---

**1948** | Born in Birmingham | John Michael Osbourne is born on December 3, 1948, in the Aston district of Birmingham, England. He is the fourth of six children; father Jack is a toolmaker, mother Lilian works at a Lucas factory. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1953–1963** | Working-class childhood in Aston | Struggles with dyslexia at school and picks up the nickname "Ozzy" in elementary school. Later speaks publicly about being bullied. | https://www.britannica.com/biography/Ozzy-Osbourne

**1963** | Hears The Beatles | At age 14, hearing The Beatles' "She Loves You" inspires him to pursue music as a career. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1964–1965** | Leaves school, takes odd jobs | Leaves school around age 15/16; works as a construction laborer, plumber's apprentice, toolmaker, car-factory worker, and briefly in a slaughterhouse. | https://www.britannica.com/biography/Ozzy-Osbourne

**1966** | Burglary conviction and prison | Convicted of burglary around age 17 and serves roughly six weeks at Winson Green Prison in Birmingham after his father refuses to pay the fine. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1967** | Joins Rare Breed | Late in the year, joins Geezer Butler's band Rare Breed as singer; the two quickly leave to form a new group. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1968** | Polka Tulk / Earth formed | Osbourne, Butler, guitarist Tony Iommi, and drummer Bill Ward come together in Birmingham under the names Polka Tulk Blues Band and then Earth. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1969** | Renamed Black Sabbath | The band changes its name to Black Sabbath in August 1969; first gig under that name on August 30, 1969, in Workington; signs to Philips/Vertigo in November. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1970** | Heavy metal is born | Debut album *Black Sabbath* is released on Friday, February 13, 1970; follow-up *Paranoid* lands in September 1970 and hits No. 1 in the UK. Both are widely credited with inventing heavy metal. | https://ultimateclassicrock.com/black-sabbath-1970-debut/

**1971** | *Master of Reality* | Third album released in July 1971, going platinum and cementing the band's doom-laden sound. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1972** | *Vol. 4* | Released September 1972; originally to be titled *Snowblind*. Band now at the height of commercial power. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1973** | *Sabbath Bloody Sabbath* | Fifth studio album released December 1973; widely considered one of their strongest records. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1975** | *Sabotage* | Sixth album released in July 1975; recorded amid lengthy management/legal disputes. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1976** | *Technical Ecstasy* | Released October 1976 to mixed reviews; internal tensions within the band intensify. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_Ecstasy

**1977** | Brief departure | Late 1977 Osbourne abruptly quits Black Sabbath just before they enter the studio for the next album; he rejoins in January 1978. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1978** | *Never Say Die!* + Van Halen tour | Eighth Sabbath album released September 29, 1978 — last studio LP with the original lineup until 2013. The Never Say Die! Tour runs May 16 – December 11, 1978, with a young Van Halen as opener. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Never_Say_Die!_Tour_(Black_Sabbath)

**1979** | Fired from Black Sabbath | On April 27, 1979, Sabbath fire Osbourne, citing chronic alcohol and drug problems; Ronnie James Dio replaces him. Later that year, signs with Jet Records and begins assembling a solo band with Sharon Arden managing day-to-day. Randy Rhoads auditions and joins in September/November 1979. | https://ultimateclassicrock.com/ozzy-osbourne-fired-from-black-sabbath/

**1980** | *Blizzard of Ozz* | Debut solo album released September 1980 (UK); featuring Randy Rhoads, Bob Daisley, Lee Kerslake. Contains "Crazy Train" and "Mr. Crowley." Eventually certified 4x platinum in the US. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blizzard_of_Ozz

**1981** | *Diary of a Madman* + dove incident | Second solo album released November 1981. During a CBS Records meeting earlier in the year, Osbourne bites the head off a live dove — a widely reported PR stunt gone rogue. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1982** | Bat incident, Alamo arrest, Randy Rhoads dies | January 20, 1982: bites the head off a bat (thought to be rubber) thrown on stage at Veterans Memorial Auditorium, Des Moines, Iowa; given rabies shots. February 19, 1982: arrested in San Antonio for urinating on the Alamo Cenotaph while wearing one of Sharon's dresses; banned from the city for a decade. March 19, 1982: Randy Rhoads, 25, killed in a small-plane crash at Flying Baron Estates in Leesburg, Florida. July 4, 1982: marries Sharon Arden in Maui, Hawaii. | https://www.history.com/this-day-in-history/january-20/ozzy-osbourne-bites-bat-head ; https://loudwire.com/ozzy-osbourne-arrested-urinating-alamo-cenotaph-anniversary/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randy_Rhoads

**1983** | *Bark at the Moon* + daughter Aimee born | New guitarist Jake E. Lee joins; *Bark at the Moon* released November/December 1983, going multi-platinum. Daughter Aimee Osbourne born. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1984** | Daughter Kelly born | Kelly Osbourne born October 27, 1984. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1985** | Live Aid reunion + son Jack born | Original Black Sabbath lineup briefly reunites for Live Aid at Philadelphia's JFK Stadium on July 13, 1985 — their first reunion since 1978. Son Jack Osbourne born November 8, 1985. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**1986** | *The Ultimate Sin* | Fourth solo album released February 1986; becomes his highest-charting US release at the time, certified platinum in May 1986. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1987** | *Tribute* to Randy Rhoads | Live album *Tribute*, recorded in 1981 with Randy Rhoads, released in 1987 to mark the fifth anniversary of Rhoads' death. Jake E. Lee is let go. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1988** | *No Rest for the Wicked* + Zakk Wylde arrives | Fifth solo album released October 1988, introducing guitarist Zakk Wylde. Osbourne appears in *The Decline of Western Civilization Part II: The Metal Years*. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1989** | "Close My Eyes Forever" + Moscow Peace Festival | Duet with Lita Ford reaches No. 8 on the Billboard Hot 100 — his highest-charting US single at that point. Performs at the Moscow Music Peace Festival in August 1989. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1991** | *No More Tears* | Sixth solo album released September 1991, featuring "Mama, I'm Coming Home" and the title track. Certified 4x platinum in the US. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne_discography

**1992** | "No More Tours" retirement tour | Announces retirement and launches the No More Tours tour; ends with a two-night reunion with Sabbath bandmates Iommi, Butler, and Ward in Costa Mesa, CA (November 14–15, 1992). | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No_More_Tours_Tour

**1993** | *Live & Loud* | Live double album released June 28, 1993; certified 4x platinum. Brief retirement doesn't hold. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne_discography

**1995** | *Ozzmosis* | Seventh solo studio album released October 1995; reaches No. 4 on the Billboard 200 and is certified 2x platinum. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1996** | Ozzfest launches | After Lollapalooza rejects him, Sharon Osbourne creates Ozzfest; inaugural dates October 25–26, 1996 in Phoenix, AZ and Devore, CA. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1997** | Black Sabbath reunion at Ozzfest | Osbourne, Iommi, and Butler (with Mike Bordin on drums) reunite for Ozzfest. In December 1997 all four original members reconvene for live recordings at Birmingham's NEC. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**1998** | *Reunion* live album | Sabbath's *Reunion* released October 1998, capturing the Birmingham NEC shows; wins a Grammy in 2000 for "Iron Man." | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**2001** | *Down to Earth* | Eighth solo album released October 16, 2001 — his first new studio material in six years; eventually certified platinum. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2002** | *The Osbournes* premieres + Queen's Jubilee | *The Osbournes* reality show debuts on MTV on March 5, 2002, becoming the network's most-watched series to that point and winning the Primetime Emmy for Outstanding Reality Program. In June 2002 performs "Paranoid" at Buckingham Palace for Queen Elizabeth II's Golden Jubilee. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Osbournes

**2003** | "Changes" hits No. 1 + quad bike crash | Duet of "Changes" with Kelly Osbourne hits UK No. 1 in December 2003 (33 years after his previous UK No. 1 with "Paranoid"). On December 8, 2003, Osbourne is critically injured in an ATV/quad bike accident at his Jordans, Buckinghamshire estate — broken collarbone, eight ribs, vertebra, punctured lung; bodyguard Sam Ruston resuscitates him; eight-day coma follows. | https://ultimateclassicrock.com/ozzy-atv-accident/

**2005** | *The Osbournes* ends + *Under Cover* | Series finale of *The Osbournes* airs March 21, 2005. Covers album *Under Cover* released November 2005. Inducted into the UK Music Hall of Fame. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Osbournes

**2006** | Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (with Sabbath) | Black Sabbath inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame on March 13, 2006. Receives a plaque on Birmingham's Walk of Stars on July 6, 2006. | https://rockhall.com/inductees/black-sabbath/

**2007** | *Black Rain* | Ninth solo album released May 22, 2007; reaches No. 3 in the US and No. 8 in the UK. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2009** | Zakk Wylde out, Gus G in | In July 2009 Osbourne says he's seeking a new guitarist; Greek player Gus G is announced in August 2009 and debuts at BlizzCon. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2010** | *Scream* | Tenth solo album (originally titled *Soul Sucka*) released June 2010; No. 4 on the Billboard 200. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2011** | Reconciles with Iommi + *God Bless Ozzy Osbourne* doc | Documentary *God Bless Ozzy Osbourne* premieres at the Tribeca Film Festival in April 2011; Osbourne reconciles with Tony Iommi and a second Black Sabbath reunion is announced. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2012** | Sabbath reunion show | May 19, 2012: reunited Black Sabbath play the O2 Academy Birmingham, their first reunion concert in the lineup's home city. Drummer Bill Ward withdraws from the reunion over contract dispute. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**2013** | *13* tops charts worldwide | Black Sabbath's *13* released June 11, 2013 — their first studio album with Osbourne since 1978 — debuts at No. 1 in the UK, US, and multiple other countries. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**2014** | MTV Europe Global Icon Award | Receives the Global Icon Award at the MTV EMAs. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2015** | Ivor Novello Lifetime Achievement | Honored with the Ivor Novello Award for Lifetime Achievement. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2016** | "The End" farewell tour begins | Black Sabbath launch their farewell tour "The End" in January 2016. *Ozzy & Jack's World Detour* premieres on History Channel July 24, 2016. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**2017** | Black Sabbath's final show | February 4, 2017: Black Sabbath play their final concert at Genting Arena in Birmingham, closing out "The End" tour. In November 2017 Osbourne announces his own *No More Tours II* farewell run. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sabbath

**2018** | *No More Tours II* begins | Launches *No More Tours II* on May 8, 2018 in Santiago, Chile. Hosts the 2008 BRIT Awards earlier and is later sidelined by a staph-infection surgery in October 2018; remaining North American dates postponed. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No_More_Tours_II

**2019** | Fall at home halts tour | Suffers a serious fall at his LA home in February 2019, aggravating old quad-bike injuries and requiring neck/spine surgery; European leg of *No More Tours II* postponed. Featured on Post Malone's "Take What You Want" (September 2019), his first US Top 10 single in 30 years. | https://wmmr.com/listicle/ozzy-osbourne-health-issues-timeline-since-2018/

**2020** | Parkinson's diagnosis revealed + *Ordinary Man* | On January 21, 2020, Osbourne reveals on *Good Morning America* he was diagnosed in February 2019 with a rare genetic form of Parkinson's disease known as PRKN2 (Parkin). *Ordinary Man* — his first solo album in nearly a decade — released February 21, 2020. *No More Tours II* shows canceled as COVID-19 hits. | https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ozzy-osbourne-reveals-parkinsons-disease-diagnosis-today-2020-01-21/

**2022** | *Patient Number 9* + Commonwealth Games | On August 30, 2022, performs "Paranoid" and "Iron Man" with Tony Iommi at the closing ceremony of the Commonwealth Games in Birmingham. *Patient Number 9* released September 9, 2022. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne

**2023** | Retires from touring | February 1, 2023: announces he is retiring from touring after failing to recover sufficiently from multiple surgeries, canceling planned 2023 dates. Reveals a further successful spinal surgery later in the year. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No_More_Tours_II

**2024** | Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (solo) | October 19, 2024: inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a solo artist — his second induction after Black Sabbath in 2006. Jack Black delivers the induction speech. | https://rockhall.com/inductees/ozzy-osbourne/

**2025** | *Back to the Beginning* farewell show + death | July 5, 2025: headlines *Back to the Beginning* at Villa Park in Aston, Birmingham — the final-ever concert by the original Black Sabbath lineup (Osbourne, Iommi, Butler, Ward). Osbourne performs seated on a throne due to Parkinson's; an all-star bill includes Metallica, Slayer, Pantera, Gojira, Alice in Chains, Halestorm, Lamb of God, Anthrax, Mastodon, with Tom Morello as musical director. Over 40,000 attend; livestream peaks at ~5.8 million. Proceeds go to Cure Parkinson's, Birmingham Children's Hospital, and Acorn Children's Hospice. **July 22, 2025:** Ozzy Osbourne dies at age 76, seventeen days after the farewell show; family says he passed "surrounded by love" at home. Cause of death not publicly confirmed at time of announcement (some later reports reference coronary artery disease — flagged as disputed/unverified here). July 30, 2025: public funeral procession through Birmingham, with the Bostin' Brass band playing Sabbath classics along Broad Street; family stops at the Black Sabbath Bridge memorial before a private service. October 7, 2025: posthumous autobiography *Last Rites* published. | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Back_to_the_Beginning ; https://variety.com/2025/music/obituaries-people-news/ozzy-osbourne-dead-black-sabbath-1236467110/ ; https://www.cnn.com/2025/07/30/entertainment/ozzy-osbourne-funeral-birmingham
