# 02 — Rolling Stone Tier: Major Music Journalism on Ozzy Osbourne

**Research scope:** Top-tier music and mainstream press coverage of Ozzy Osbourne — Rolling Stone, The Guardian, NME, Spin, Kerrang!, Louder (Classic Rock / Metal Hammer), Loudwire, MOJO, Billboard, and NYT. Focus on the most-cited album reviews, profile interviews, career retrospectives, coverage of his July 22, 2025 death, the Back to the Beginning farewell show (July 5, 2025), and landmark controversies (bat incident, Alamo).

Compiled 2026-04-15. All URLs verified live via web fetch/search. Direct quotes pulled from the primary sources where accessible.

---

## Top Findings

### 1. Rolling Stone — Obituary (the definitive US obit)
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone (US)
- **Title:** "Ozzy Osbourne, Black Sabbath Singer and Heavy Metal Pioneer, Dead at 76"
- **Author:** Kory Grow
- **Date:** July 22, 2025
- **Summary:** The anchor American obit. Frames Ozzy as both the frontman who "virtually invented heavy metal" with Black Sabbath and "a reality-TV pioneer" in his second act. Notes that between his solo and Sabbath catalog, Osbourne was the single most-represented artist on Rolling Stone's 100 Greatest Metal Albums of All Time list (Paranoid at No. 1, Sabbath's debut at No. 5, Blizzard of Ozz at No. 9). Cites four Grammys including a Lifetime Achievement with Sabbath.
- **Key quote (Lars Ulrich, quoted in obit, Rock Hall induction):** "the heavy-metal genre might as well be subtitled 'Music derivative of Black Sabbath.'"
- **Key quote (Ozzy, self-reflection quoted in obit):** "You couldn't write my story; you couldn't invent me."
- **Family statement:** "It is with more sadness than mere words can convey that we have to report that our beloved Ozzy Osbourne has passed away this morning. He was with his family and surrounded by love."
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ozzy-osbourne-black-sabbath-dead-obituary-1227265/

### 2. The Guardian — Alexis Petridis Tribute / Obituary (the definitive UK obit)
- **Outlet:** The Guardian
- **Title:** "Ozzy Osbourne, the people's Prince of Darkness, took heavy metal into the light"
- **Author:** Alexis Petridis (Guardian chief music critic)
- **Date:** July 22, 2025
- **Summary:** The most-cited British critical appraisal. Petridis traces Ozzy from a troubled Birmingham childhood marked by dyslexia and abuse, through petty crime, the formation of Sabbath, his 1979 firing for substance abuse, and his reinvention as a solo star after hiring Randy Rhoads (who said Ozzy was so drunk at the audition he hired him after only hearing him tune up). The piece's thesis — captured in the headline — argues Ozzy smuggled doom-metal imagery into the mainstream without ever being truly menacing: the "cartoonish Prince of Darkness" who bit the head off a dove at CBS and a live bat in Des Moines but whose public warmth ultimately made heavy metal palatable to a mass audience.
- **URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/music/2025/jul/22/ozzy-osbourne-the-peoples-prince-of-darkness-took-heavy-metal-into-the-light

### 3. Rolling Stone — Back to the Beginning Live Review (farewell concert)
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone
- **Title:** "Ozzy Osbourne Gives Earth-Shaking Farewell at Black Sabbath's Back to the Beginning"
- **Author:** Rhys Buchanan
- **Date:** July 6, 2025 (covering the July 5, 2025 show at Villa Park, Birmingham)
- **Summary:** The marquee American review of the 10-hour, 40,000-capacity farewell with a peak livestream of 5.8 million. Sabbath's original lineup (Ozzy, Tony Iommi, Geezer Butler, Bill Ward together for the first time since 2005) closed with War Pigs, N.I.B., Iron Man, and Paranoid. Ozzy, unable to walk due to advanced Parkinson's, performed a short solo set from a bat-and-skull throne before the Sabbath reunion. Tom Morello served as music director; undercard included Metallica, Slayer, Guns N' Roses members, Pantera, Tool, Anthrax, Alice in Chains, Mastodon, Gojira, Lamb of God, Halestorm.
- **Opening quote (Ozzy, from the throne):** "Let the madness begin!"
- **Ozzy to the crowd:** "It's so good to be on this fucking stage you have no idea."
- **James Hetfield tribute from the stage:** "Without Sabbath there would be no Metallica — thank you boys for giving us a purpose in life."
- **Ozzy's final stage words:** "Go fucking crazy, it's the last song."
- **Critic's image:** Ozzy "writhed and wriggled like a man summoning every last inch of the hell-raising spirit still in him."
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-live-reviews/ozzy-osbourne-black-sabbath-back-to-the-beginning-concert-1235379405/

### 4. The Guardian — Back to the Beginning Concert Review
- **Outlet:** The Guardian
- **Title:** "Black Sabbath and Ozzy Osbourne: Back to the Beginning review – all-star farewell to the gods of metal is epic and emotional"
- **Date:** July 6, 2025
- **Summary:** The UK broadsheet take. Calls Sabbath's closing four-song set (War Pigs, N.I.B., Iron Man, Paranoid) "the farewell this extraordinary band deserved," with "an undercard of stadium-fillers and festival headliners paying tribute." Praises returning drummer Bill Ward for adding "the swing other Sabbath drummers had never managed," Iommi "churning out his monstrous riffs," Butler "flitting around on bass," and Ozzy himself framed as "a baffled and discomfited force of nature" singing from his throne. Closes on the image of fireworks over Villa Park as the inventors of metal left the stage for the final time.
- **URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/music/2025/jul/06/black-sabbath-and-ozzy-osbourne-back-to-the-beginning-review-all-star-farewell-to-the-gods-of-metal-is-epic-and-emotional

### 5. Rolling Stone — Blizzard of Ozz at 40 Retrospective
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone
- **Title:** "'Blizzard of Ozz' at 40: Ozzy Osbourne Reflects on Career-Reviving Solo Debut"
- **Author:** Kory Grow
- **Summary:** The canonical retrospective on the 1980 solo debut that resurrected Ozzy's career after his Sabbath firing. Explores the Randy Rhoads partnership, the writing of "Crazy Train," "Mr. Crowley," and "Suicide Solution," and Ozzy's own assessment of the pivot away from Sabbath. Rolling Stone's original review gave the album four stars; it later landed at No. 9 on RS's 100 Greatest Metal Albums of All Time.
- **Ozzy quote:** "I was the captain of my own ship, and it was fun. I just did what I wanted."
- **Ozzy on leaving Sabbath:** "I was done with Black Sabbath. It needed me to leave or one of us to go. We were tired of fighting everything and everyone."
- **Ozzy on the anniversary:** "We had a blast. It was a long time ago now though. That was a fast 40 years. I've never seen 40 years go by so quick."
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/ozzy-osbourne-blizzard-of-ozz-interview-1061301/

### 6. Rolling Stone — The 100 Greatest Metal Albums of All Time (ranked list / canon-setter)
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone
- **Published:** 2017
- **Summary:** The single most-referenced critical ranking in the genre. Ozzy is the most represented artist on the list — appearing on Paranoid (No. 1), Black Sabbath's self-titled debut (No. 5), and his own Blizzard of Ozz (No. 9). This list anchors almost every post-death legacy piece and is cited directly in the RS obituary as evidence of Ozzy's primacy in the form.
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/the-100-greatest-metal-albums-of-all-time-113614/black-sabbath-paranoid-1970-115785/

### 7. NME — The Big Read: "This album saved my life"
- **Outlet:** NME
- **Title:** "The Big Read – Ozzy Osbourne: 'This album saved my life'"
- **Author:** Kevin EG Perry
- **Summary:** The most-cited late-career NME profile, built around 2020's Ordinary Man. Notable for being the piece in which Ozzy discussed his Parkinson's publicly in depth — he reveals he was actually diagnosed in 2003 but kept it private for 18 years. Frames the album as literal salvation after a 2019 fall and a depressive collapse.
- **Ozzy quote:** "This album is quite possibly one of the most important albums I've ever made because it saved my life."
- **Ozzy on his writing:** "I always write my best songs about death."
- **Ozzy on his wife Sharon:** "I'm lucky to have a friend in the world, never mind a wife."
- **URL:** https://www.nme.com/big-reads/the-big-read-ozzy-osbourne-interview-2612386

### 8. Rolling Stone — Bat-Biting Incident Anniversary Retrospective
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone
- **Title:** "Ozzy Osbourne Bit the Head Off a Live Bat 40 Years Ago Today"
- **Date:** January 20, 2022 (40th anniversary of the Jan 20, 1982 Des Moines show)
- **Summary:** The go-to American retelling of the single most-cited rock-and-roll controversy of Ozzy's career. At Veterans Memorial Auditorium in Des Moines during the Diary of a Madman tour, a dead bat smuggled in by a teenage fan (Mark Neal) was thrown on stage; Ozzy, believing it was rubber, bit its head off — and felt it twitch. The RS piece famously claimed the bat-thrower had never come forward publicly, though Neal had actually identified himself to the Des Moines Register the same year. Ozzy later said he had to undergo rabies shots at every tour stop afterwards.
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ozzy-osbourne-bites-head-off-bat-anniversary-1287867/

### 9. Rolling Stone — Alamo Urination Tribute
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone
- **Title:** "Ozzy Osbourne: The Alamo Shares Tribute Despite Urination Incident"
- **Date:** July 2025 (post-death coverage)
- **Summary:** Recaps the February 1982 incident in which Ozzy, in a drunken blackout and wearing one of Sharon's dresses (she had hidden his clothes to keep him in the hotel), urinated on the Alamo Cenotaph — the 60-foot monument to the Texans killed in 1836. He was arrested for public intoxication, freed on $40 bond, and banned from San Antonio until 1992, when he publicly apologized and donated $10,000 to the Daughters of the Republic of Texas. The 2025 piece is notable because the Alamo's official account posted a tribute upon his death, writing that they "honor history in all its complexities."
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ozzy-osbourne-alamo-tribute-urination-incident-1235392155/

### 10. Louder (Classic Rock / Metal Hammer) — Back to the Beginning "Ultimate Review"
- **Outlet:** Louder Sound (Classic Rock / Metal Hammer umbrella)
- **Title:** "'It's not until Ozzy's actually there, thanking fans and getting tearful, that it sinks in.' Black Sabbath's Back To The Beginning: our ultimate review of their triumphant final show"
- **Date:** July 2025
- **Summary:** The metal-press review of record. Describes the 10-hour farewell as "amongst the biggest and most important metal gigs of all time." Standout moments: Halestorm's rendition of "Perry Mason," and a cross-band drum summit on "Supernaut" featuring Mastodon's Brann Dailor, Tool's Danny Carey, Gojira's Mario Duplantier, and Slipknot's Eloy Casagrande. The show opened and closed with the sounds of rain and tolling bells — a direct callback to the opening of Sabbath's 1970 debut.
- **Ozzy to the Villa Park crowd (quoted):** "You've got no fucking idea what this means to me."
- **URL:** https://www.loudersound.com/bands-artists/concerts-shows/black-sabbath-ozzy-osbourne-back-to-the-beginning-review

### 11. Kerrang! — Final Show Announcement & Live Review
- **Outlet:** Kerrang!
- **Pieces:** (a) "Black Sabbath announce colossal final show with Ozzy at Villa Park" (announcement), and (b) "Live review: Back To The Beginning, Villa Park" (concert review).
- **Summary:** Kerrang! — metal's home-field magazine — ran the announcement of the Tom Morello-curated farewell and followed with the on-the-ground review. Also produced Alice Cooper's much-quoted tribute from his Cardiff show the night of Ozzy's death: "The whole world is mourning Ozzy tonight."
- **URLs:**

  - Alice Cooper tribute: https://www.kerrang.com/alice-cooper-utilita-arena-cardiff-wales-july-22-ozzy-osbourne-tribute-black-sabbath-cultural-icon-rocknroll-legend

### 12. Spin — 2023 Late-Career Interview & 1986 Cover Story
- **Outlet:** Spin
- **Pieces:** (a) "Ozzy Osbourne On Health, Podcasts, Reality TV And A Half-Century Of Rock" (Dec 2023), and (b) the 1986 cover story "Oz the Great and Terrible."
- **Summary:** The 2023 Spin interview is one of the last long-form profiles before his death, covering The Osbournes Podcast, his framing as "godfather of reality TV," and his ongoing health. The 1986 cover story "Oz the Great and Terrible" is the archival Spin piece most commonly cited from his peak commercial era, catching him 18 years into his career with his famous quote that it was "bigger than ever."
- **URLs:**
  - 2023: https://www.spin.com/2023/12/ozzy-osbourne-interview/
  - 1986: https://www.spinmagazine.com/featured/ozzy-osbourne-september-1986-cover-story-oz-the-great-and-terrible/

### 13. Rolling Stone — Funeral Coverage (Birmingham, July 30, 2025)
- **Outlet:** Rolling Stone
- **Title:** "'He Was Rock Royalty': Ozzy Osbourne Fans Gather for Emotional Farewell at Funeral"
- **Summary:** The on-the-ground report from Birmingham's public farewell procession — covered alongside NPR's "England's Birmingham bids farewell to Ozzy Osbourne, its homegrown heavy metal hero." Both pieces document the city's civic embrace of a hometown son.
- **URL:** https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/ozzy-osbourne-funeral-birmingham-1235396998/

### 14. MOJO — Collectors Series Tribute Issue
- **Outlet:** MOJO (UK)
- **Title:** "MOJO Collectors Series: Ozzy Osbourne Changes 1948–2025 + Black Sabbath Special"
- **Summary:** MOJO's dedicated commemorative — the British music-heritage magazine's long-form tribute edition tracking his full Birmingham-to-global-icon arc. The standard deep-dive format the magazine is known for, serving as the archival long-read companion to the day-of news obits.
