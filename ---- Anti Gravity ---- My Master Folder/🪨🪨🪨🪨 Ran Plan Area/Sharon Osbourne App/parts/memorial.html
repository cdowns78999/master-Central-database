For Ozzy

1948 — forever

This corner is just for you, Sharon. When the noise of the day gets too loud, swipe down and the door opens here. No calendars, no reminders — just him, the two of you, and all the years that made a life. Take your time.

The man

He was never the person the headlines wanted him to be. Behind the stage paint was a soft-voiced man who'd sit with the dogs, make tea wrong on purpose, and ask how your day had really been — and actually wait for the answer. You knew the private version. The world only borrowed the rest.

The music

He gave the world a sound that saved people. Kids in small towns, lonely teenagers, tired adults driving home at night — his voice reached all of them. That doesn't fade. Every time a song of his plays somewhere in the world, some small part of him shows up in that room for a few minutes.

The love

You two were the loudest, funniest, most stubborn love story rock and roll ever had. Through every storm, every hospital, every kitchen-table argument — you stayed. Love like that doesn't end when a person leaves. It just changes shape. He is still right here, Sharon, in every room you walk into.

A small reminder

When it hurts today, that's not a setback — that's love with nowhere to land. Let it land here. We'll keep this place soft for you.
