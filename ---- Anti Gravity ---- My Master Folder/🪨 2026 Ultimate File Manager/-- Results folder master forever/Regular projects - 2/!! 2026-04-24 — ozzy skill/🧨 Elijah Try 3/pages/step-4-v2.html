05

What an Artist Walks Away With

core takeaways
  • Per-stream rates are averages, not contractsSpotify ~$0.003–$0.004, Apple ~$0.006–$0.008, Amazon ~$0.0088. Your actual cut depends on the pool size that month.
  • The pool moves every monthMSPS recalculates each period — your share = your streams ÷ total platform streams × payable pool.
  • Below 1,000 streams = $0Spotify's annual gate sweeps sub-threshold tracks into the pool. Get past 1,000 streams in a 12-month window.
  • 30 seconds = 1 streamAnything shorter doesn't count. Build hooks early so the count actually triggers.
  • The DSP pays your rights holder, not youLabel or distributor receives the wire. Your contract decides what trickles down.
  • Premium tier streams pay more than ad-supportedSame artist, same song — premium ARPU is higher, so the pool slice is bigger.
  • Distros take 0–15% off the topMost "free" tiers cap at 9–15%, paid tiers (DistroKid, TuneCore) keep all royalties for a flat fee.
  • Don't game it — fraud detection is fastApple killed 2B streams in 2025; Spotify removed 75M AI tracks. Royalty clawback + removal is the price.

Pool Math

MSPS · Pro-Rata

Concept · Pool Math

Why this matters

The market-share payment system (MSPS) pools all subscription + ad revenue, then distributes based on each artist's share of total streams in that period. Your rate fluctuates monthly with the size of the pool.

  • Pool = 65–70% of total DSP revenue (Royalty Exchange)
  • Your share = your streams ÷ total platform streams
  • Per-stream rate is a backwards-calculated average, not a contract

1,000-Stream Gate

Annual Threshold

Concept · The Gate

Why this matters

A track must hit at least 1,000 streams in a 12-month period to generate any royalties on Spotify. Below this threshold, earnings are pooled and distributed to popular artists.

  • Gate is per-track, not per-artist
  • Resets on a 12-month rolling window
  • Sub-1k revenue is redistributed to the top of the pool — it doesn't disappear, but you don't see it

Premium > Ad

Tier Matters

Concept · Tier Math

Why this matters

A stream is counted after 30 seconds of playback. Premium subscriber streams generate more royalty value than ad-supported streams due to higher revenue per user.

  • Premium ARPU is multiples higher than ad ARPU
  • Same song, same artist — premium listeners feed a richer pool
  • Territory matters too: US/UK/EU pools pay more per stream than developing markets
