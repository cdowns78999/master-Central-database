Standard booking agent

STANDARD BOOKING AGENT

Standard booking agent commission is 10–15% of the artist's gross performance fee (guarantee + backend), with 10% most common for mid-to-major acts.

VENUE TIERS BY

Venue tiers by capacity: DIY/club (100–500 cap), mid-size club (500–1,500), theater (1,500–5,000), amphitheater (5,000–18,000), arena (10,000–20,000+), stadium (40,000+).

Deal structures

DEAL STRUCTURES

Deal structures: flat guarantee (artist is paid regardless of ticket sales), guarantee vs. door split (artist takes whichever is higher), or guarantee + backend (e.g., 85/15 after the venue recoups costs).

What an artist walks away with

core takeaways
  • Standard booking agent commissionStandard booking agent commission is 10–15% of the artist's gross performance fee (guarantee + backend), with 10% most common for mid-to-major acts.
  • Venue tiers by capacityVenue tiers by capacity: DIY/club (100–500 cap), mid-size club (500–1,500), theater (1,500–5,000), amphitheater (5,000–18,000), arena (10,000–20,000+), stadium (40,000+).
  • Deal structuresDeal structures: flat guarantee (artist is paid regardless of ticket sales), guarantee vs. door split (artist takes whichever is higher), or guarantee + backend (e.g., 85/15 after the venue recoups costs).
  • The big four bookingThe big four booking agencies — CAA, WME, UTA, and Paradigm/Wasserman — handle the vast majority of major touring acts. Independent boutique agencies (Arrival Artists, Ground Control Touring) serve emerging and niche genres.
  • Avoid: Signing with an agentSigning with an agent who takes upfront fees — legitimate booking agents are commission-only.
  • Avoid: Accepting a flat guaranteeAccepting a flat guarantee without a backend clause when the artist has real ticket-buying demand — this leaves money on the table at sold-out shows.
  • Avoid: Not reviewing the technicalNot reviewing the technical rider requirements against the venue's actual capacity and budget, leading to production shortfalls on tour.
