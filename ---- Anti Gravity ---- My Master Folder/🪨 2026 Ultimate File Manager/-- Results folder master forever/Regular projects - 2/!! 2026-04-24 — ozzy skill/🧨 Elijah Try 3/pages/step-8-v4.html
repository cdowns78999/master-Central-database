Live Performance 101

Touring is a math problem dressed up as art — read the deal shape, stack merch on top, and route the map like a loop instead of a zigzag.

  1. Know the three deal shapes cold Guarantee (fixed, promoter risk), door deal (70–85% of door, shared risk), greater-of (floor + upside). Every offer reduces to one of these three — name it before you sign.
  2. A $100 ticket nets $8–$15 — merch is the lifeline After venue, ticketing, production, and crew, the artist sees ~$8–$15 of every $100 ticket. ~$15/fan in merch is often the difference between break-even and going home in debt.
  3. Build local before national, treat early tours as investment Touring before a local fanbase exists creates empty rooms and venues that won't rebook. Budget the first national run as audience-building, not revenue.
Next move Pull your last (or next) show offer and label the deal shape, then forecast merch-per-fan against the cost stack — if the gap doesn't close, the run isn't ready yet.
