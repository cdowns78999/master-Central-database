  • Sunset clauseSunset clause: a standard contractual provision that gradually reduces the manager's commission percentage on deals made during their tenure after the contract ends — typically declining to zero over 3–5 years post-term. This protects arti…
  • Avoid: Signing a management dealSigning a management deal too early — before you have consistent income, a body of work, and clear career direction, giving up 15–20% of gross income to a manager provides limited ROI.
  • Avoid: Not including a sunsetNot including a sunset clause — without one, a former manager continues collecting full commission on deals they made during their tenure indefinitely, sometimes for decades.

Standard commission rate

STANDARD COMMISSION RATE

Reference · Key Fact

Why this matters

Standard commission rate: 15–20% of gross income. Established managers with strong track records may command 20–25%. Some managers work on net income (after deducted expenses), which is significantly more favorable for the artist. Most successful management relationships begin informally — a trusted person from the artist's existing network (friend, family member, fellow musician) starts managing before either party formalizes the arrangement.
