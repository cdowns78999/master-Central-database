Ozzy Osbourne

The Fall — 2019 to 2020

A practical quarterly breakdown of the hardest stretch of his career.

In early 2019, Ozzy fell at his Los Angeles home and aggravated an old neck injury. What followed was surgery, pneumonia, cancelled tours, a Parkinson’s diagnosis revealed on Good Morning America in January 2020, and the release of his first solo album in a decade. The page below breaks it down quarter by quarter, sourced from his own interviews and official statements.

EXHIBIT B · Q1 2019
Q1 / 2019

Q1 2019 — Jan–MarThe fall at home. Hospital admission. Tour postponements.

Q1 2019 THE FALL — LOS ANGELES HOME

Report filed: subject sustained a fall at his Los Angeles residence during nighttime hours in early 2019. Evidence suggests the impact aggravated a prior cervical and spinal injury dating to a 2003 quad-bike incident. Hardware from the original repair was disturbed. The fall reset the trajectory of the entire year. [Src 1] [Src 6]

Q1 2019 HOSPITALIZATION — FLU COMPLICATIONS

On the record: subject hospitalized with flu and pneumonia complications during the same window. Reports indicate a staph infection compounding the respiratory illness. The combined load on a body already carrying spinal trauma kept him under medical supervision longer than initial estimates suggested. [Src 3] [Src 6]

Q1 2019 TOUR DATES POSTPONED

Statement released February through March 2019: No More Tours 2 European and select North American dates postponed. Promoters cited medical recovery. The schedule was not cancelled outright — the file stayed open, dates rolled forward, and the road remained the assumed destination. [Src 3]

EXHIBIT C · Q2 2019
Q2 / 2019

Q2 2019 — Apr–JunSurgery. Recovery. Family takes the podium.

Q2 2019 NECK SURGERY — APRIL 2019

Report filed April 2019: subject underwent neck and spine surgery to address dislodged metal hardware from the 2003 quad-bike injury. The procedure removed and reset components that had shifted during the fall. Evidence suggests this was the central medical event of the year, not the fall itself. [Src 1] [Src 6]

Q2 2019 EXTENDED HOME RECOVERY

On the record: recovery continued at the Los Angeles residence through spring into summer. Additional 2019 tour dates were rolled into 2020. The pattern shifted from short pause to indefinite hold. Touring infrastructure stood down while the subject remained off his feet. [Src 3]

Q2 2019 FAMILY HANDLES COMMUNICATIONS

Witness Sharon Osbourne confirmed the family assumed responsibility for public statements during the most acute recovery window. Updates flowed through her, not the subject. The arrangement held through the period when the patient was least able to speak for himself on camera or in print. [Src 1]

EXHIBIT D · Q3 2019
Q3 / 2019

Q3 2019 — Jul–SepWalking again. Studio reawakens. Post Malone calls.

Q3 2019 RECOVERY MILESTONES

Evidence suggests measurable progress through the summer months. Subject was walking again. Vocal work resumed in limited sessions. The body that had been horizontal for months began returning to upright function. Each milestone was logged quietly, away from the press cycle. [Src 2] [Src 6]

Q3 2019 POST MALONE COLLAB — SEPT 6

On the record September 6, 2019: “Take What You Want” released with Post Malone and Travis Scott. The track reconnected the subject with producer Andrew Watt. The session would prove to be the hinge of the comeback file. A guest verse opened a door no one had been knocking on. [Src 5]

Q3 2019 ORDINARY MAN SESSIONS BEGIN

Report filed: Ordinary Man recording sessions ramped up in Los Angeles. Watt produced. Duff McKagan and Chad Smith logged in as the rhythm section. The pace was fast by the subject’s standards — short sessions, full takes, minimal overdubs. The album was being built in plain sight. [Src 4]

EXHIBIT E · Q4 2019
Q4 / 2019

Q4 2019 — Oct–DecQuiet build. Singles drop. Family prepares disclosure.

Q4 2019 UNDER THE GRAVEYARD — NOV 8

Statement released November 8, 2019: “Under the Graveyard” issued as the lead single. The first new solo material in years arrived without fanfare and without the subject on a stage. The single did the work the tour could not. [Src 4]

Q4 2019 STRAIGHT TO HELL — SLASH TEASE

On the record late Q4 2019: “Straight to Hell” featuring Slash was teased ahead of the album. The pairing signaled that the record would not be a quiet recovery document. Heavier material was waiting in the queue. [Src 4]

Q4 2019 FAMILY PREPARES DISCLOSURE

Evidence suggests the family was privately preparing for a public Parkinson’s disclosure planned for January. The diagnosis itself had been confirmed earlier in the year. The disclosure window was chosen — not stumbled into. The interview was on the calendar before the new year began. [Src 1]

EXHIBIT F · Q1 2020
Q1 / 2020

Q1 2020 — Jan–MarThe reveal. The album. The press tour.

Q1 2020 GMA REVEAL — JAN 21

Report filed January 21, 2020: subject and Sharon Osbourne sat with Robin Roberts on Good Morning America and disclosed a PRKN2 (Parkin 2) Parkinson’s diagnosis, confirmed February 2019. The interview placed a name on the silence of the prior year. The case file was now public. [Src 1]

Q1 2020 ORDINARY MAN — FEB 21

On the record February 21, 2020: Ordinary Man released on Epic. First solo album in ten years. Featured Elton John, Post Malone, Slash, and Tom Morello across the tracklist. The record arrived one month after the diagnosis went public — a sequence that read as deliberate. [Src 4]

Q1 2020 PRESS TOUR — STERN, ROLLING STONE

Statement released across multiple outlets: Rolling Stone cover, Howard Stern sit-down, SiriusXM appearances. The subject talked openly about the fall, the surgery, and the diagnosis. The press tour functioned as both album promotion and public reintroduction. [Src 2] [Src 6]

Q2 2020 — Apr–JunPandemic pause. Reflection instead of roadwork.

Q2 2020 PANDEMIC CANCELS TOUR

Report filed: COVID-19 forced the cancellation of the rebooked 2020 No More Tours 2 dates. The schedule that had been postponed twice was now off the calendar entirely. The road, which had been the assumed destination since early 2019, was no longer available as a return route. [Src 3]

Q2 2020 ISOLATION ON THE RECORD

On the record: subject spoke publicly about how the lockdown forced him to confront recovery rather than tour through it. Evidence suggests the pause that frustrated the industry served the patient. There was nowhere to be except home, healing. [Src 6]

Q3 / 2020

Q3 2020 — Jul–SepQuiet work. Therapy. New music taking shape.

Q3 2020 PHYSICAL THERAPY CONTINUES

Report filed: continued physical therapy and recovery work from the 2019 surgery through the summer months. The rehabilitation arc that began on a hospital bed in spring 2019 was now eighteen months long. Progress was measured in small increments, logged privately. [Src 2] [Src 6]

Q3 2020 WATT SESSIONS — HINTED

Evidence suggests the working relationship with Andrew Watt did not pause. Interview hints across the period indicated new music was already being tracked. The Ordinary Man session model — fast, focused, collaborative — was being run again, quietly. [Src 4]

EXHIBIT I · Q4 2020
Q4 / 2020

Q4 2020 — Oct–DecYear-end reflection. Survival logged. Case remains open.

Q4 2020 GRATITUDE ON THE RECORD

Statement released across year-end interviews: subject emphasized gratitude and survival. The tone had shifted from the bewildered patient of early 2019 to someone who had counted the costs and was still here. The phrasing was consistent across outlets. [Src 4]

Q4 2020 TOURING MODEL ABANDONED

Witness Sharon Osbourne confirmed the family was signaling a shift away from grueling world tours toward album-focused work. The decision closed a chapter that had defined fifty years of the subject’s professional life. The record, not the road, was now the unit of work. [Src 6]

Q4 2020 FOUNDATION FOR PATIENT NO. 9

Report filed: the conditions for the 2022 Patient Number 9 era were laid down in this quarter. Studio model proven, touring expectation retired, public narrative settled. Evidence suggests every choice in late 2020 fed the next album cycle. The case was not closing. It was reorganizing. [Src 1] [Src 6]

Recap

Ozzy survived the fall. The April 2019 surgery reset dislodged hardware from a 2003 quad-bike injury. The Parkinson’s diagnosis, confirmed in February 2019, was made public a year later on Good Morning America. Touring did not resume — pandemic cancellations closed that door — but studio work continued with producer Andrew Watt, leading directly to the 2022 album Patient Number 9.
