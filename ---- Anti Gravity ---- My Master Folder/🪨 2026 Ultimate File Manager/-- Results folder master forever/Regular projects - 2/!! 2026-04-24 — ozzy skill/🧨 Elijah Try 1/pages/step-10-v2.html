    1 spike beats 10

    ONE SPIKE BEATS TEN AVERAGES

    Reference · Key Concept

    Why this matters

    "Every artist needs a primary spike — the one area you go deeper than anyone around you." Ten lessons in, the next move isn't more breadth. It's depth in one chosen lane. Breadth is table stakes; depth is leverage.

    • One spike differentiates you from every other foundationally-competent artist.
    • Pick from the 10 spike chips below the tree — Money, Ownership, Audience, Operator, Global, Story, Legal, DIY, Touring, Hybrid-Modern.
    • Commit for at least 90 days before evaluating.

    Depth > breadth

    DEPTH BEATS BREADTH

    Reference · Key Concept

    Why this matters

    "Don't try to master all 10 at once. Pick one spike from the list and go deep for the next 6 months — depth in one area beats shallow across many." A deep well of skill in one area attracts collaborators and opportunities; ten shallow wells produce nothing reusable.

    • Depth compounds — each month of focused work makes the next month cheaper.
    • Breadth dilutes — splitting attention 10 ways means each piece runs at 10% intensity.
    • You can always add a second spike after the first is established.

    Audit weakest

    AUDIT YOUR WEAKEST STEP

    Reference · Key Concept

    Why this matters

    "The 9 prior steps cover the foundation. Walk through each step and ask: where am I weakest? That's your next 90-day project." The audit is the cheapest, lowest-stakes way to identify your real next move without guessing — and the answer is usually the area you've been quietly avoiding.

    • "The biggest career bottleneck is usually the area you're most uncomfortable looking at — that's exactly where to start."
    • Branding, Recording, Distribution, Streaming, Royalties, Publishing, Social, Live, Team — score yourself 1-5 on each.
    • Whichever scores lowest is your next quarter's project.
