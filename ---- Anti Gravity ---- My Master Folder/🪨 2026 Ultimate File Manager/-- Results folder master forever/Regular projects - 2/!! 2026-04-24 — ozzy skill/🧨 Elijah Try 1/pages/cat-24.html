Correct hiring order

CORRECT HIRING ORDER

Reference · Key Fact

Why this matters

Correct hiring order for most emerging artists: (1) Entertainment attorney first — you need legal protection before signing anything; (2) Manager second — once you have traction and need career strategy; (3) Booking agent third — once the manager can demonstrate demand to agents; (4) Publicist fourth — around a release with genuine cultural hook; (5) Business manager last — once income justifies professional financial management. The manager is the most impactful team member and the one artists most often acquire incorrectly.

Manager commission

MANAGER COMMISSION

Reference · Key Fact

Why this matters

Manager commission: 15–20% of gross income across all revenue streams (recordings, live, merch, sync, endorsements). Some managers charge 20% across the board; more artist-friendly agreements exclude merch or touring revenue from the commission base. Commission-only means the manager only earns when the artist earns — aligning incentives. The manager is the most impactful team member and the one artists most often acquire incorrectly.

Entertainment attorney fees

ENTERTAINMENT ATTORNEY FEES

Reference · Key Fact

Why this matters

Entertainment attorney fees: hourly rates of $250–$600/hour; some transactional work (deal review, contract negotiation) is billed at a flat fee ($500–$5,000 per transaction). Most music attorneys in major markets (LA, NYC, Nashville) charge $350–$500/hour for experienced representation. The manager is the most impactful team member and the one artists most often acquire incorrectly.
