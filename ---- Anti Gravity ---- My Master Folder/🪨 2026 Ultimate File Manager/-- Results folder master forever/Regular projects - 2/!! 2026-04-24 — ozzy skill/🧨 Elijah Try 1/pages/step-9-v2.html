Reference · Roster

The five professionals you build around

The artist's professional team is composed of five roles — manager, entertainment attorney, booking agent, publicist, and business manager — each serving a distinct function and joining at a different stage of career development.

  • Manager — career strategy, day-to-day operations, deal pipeline (15-20% of gross).
  • Entertainment Attorney — contract review, deal negotiation, IP protection ($250-600/hr).
  • Booking Agent — live show bookings, tour routing (10-15% of guarantees).
  • Publicist — press, story placement, media relations (monthly retainer).
  • Business Manager — taxes, royalty accounting, financial planning (5% or $500-3K/mo).

Reference · Sequence

Why the order is fixed

From Symphonic Blog — "Correct hiring order for most emerging artists: (1) Entertainment attorney first; (2) Manager second — once you have traction; (3) Booking agent third — once the manager can demonstrate demand; (4) Publicist fourth — articles need a story."

  • Attorney first — you need legal protection before signing anything, including the management agreement.
  • Manager second — once there's traction worth turning into strategy.
  • Booking agent third — once the manager can demonstrate ticket demand.
  • Publicist fourth — once you have a real press story to tell.

Reference · Pricing

What each role costs you

Each team member is priced differently — and the structure tells you what aligns their incentives with yours.

  • Manager — 15-20% of gross income across all streams (recordings, live, merch, sync, endorsements). Commission-only is industry standard.
  • Booking agent — 10-15% of show guarantees. Talent agency license required in CA and NY.
  • Business manager — 5% of gross or $500-3,000/month retainer. Becomes essential when annual income clears six figures.
  • Attorney — $250-600/hour, or $500-5,000 flat per transaction. Most major-market attorneys charge $350-500/hour.
