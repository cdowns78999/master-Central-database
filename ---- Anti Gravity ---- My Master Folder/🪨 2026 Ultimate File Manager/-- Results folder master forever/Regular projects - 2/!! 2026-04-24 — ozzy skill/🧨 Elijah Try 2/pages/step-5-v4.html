Royalties 101

Every song runs on two parallel pipelines — master and publishing — and you only get paid on the ones you've actually registered for.

  1. Two pipelines, always Every song generates master royalties (the recording) and publishing royalties (the composition). Separate plumbing, separate paychecks — never assume one covers the other.
  2. Register with a PRO before release Performance royalties accumulate from the moment a song goes live, but they cannot pay an unregistered writer. Sign up with ASCAP or BMI before you ship a single track.
  3. The MLC is not optional Independent artists who skip MLC or a publishing administrator leave 15–25% of their total streaming income uncollected. Register every release.
Next move Pull up your release list and confirm every track is registered with both a PRO and the MLC (or a pub admin). Anything missing — fix it today.
