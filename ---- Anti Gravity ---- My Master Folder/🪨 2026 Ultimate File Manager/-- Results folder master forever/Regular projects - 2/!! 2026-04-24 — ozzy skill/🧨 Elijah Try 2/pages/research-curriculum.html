Curriculum Outline — What a Label Runner Walks Away With

7 core competencies
  • Catalog ArchitectureHow major platforms structure their data — tracks, releases, rights, splits, ISRCs and UPCs as the backbone of every query.
  • UI That Feels Like a ToolWhat separates label-grade software from generic dashboards — density, keyboard surface, and consistent column logic.
  • Metadata StandardsWhere the industry's catalog metadata standards came from (DDEX, MWN, ISNI) and why they still leak at the edges.
  • Royalty & Rights IntegrationsHow royalty-tracking flows across providers — collection societies, neighboring rights, sync agencies, and DSP statements.
  • DIY × Platform StackHow indie labels mix DIY tools with paid platforms — Notion + Disco + Chartmetric + spreadsheets — and where the seams show.
  • Chart & Trend SignalsReading playlist movement, Shazam velocity, and TikTok creates as forward indicators rather than rear-view metrics.
  • Workflow CompressionThe iTunes-condensed pattern — moving operators from 6 tabs to 1 surface without flattening detail or losing context.

Chartmetric

Streaming insights

Reference tool · Category leader

Why operators study this UI

Chartmetric pioneered cross-DSP catalog intelligence — pulling Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, TikTok, Shazam, and radio into one searchable surface. Its data model is the de facto reference for any modern label dashboard.

  • Artist + track + playlist + audience as first-class objects
  • Time-series for every metric, not just current snapshots
  • Heavy use of saved views and side-by-side comparisons
  • Tag-based catalog grouping (genre, mood, deal status)

Soundcharts

Chart tracker

Reference tool · A&R favorite

Why operators study this UI

Soundcharts compresses dozens of country-specific charts and platform leaderboards into one comparable view, with strong filtering for early-momentum signals A&R teams pay for.

  • Country & chart filter chips above every table
  • Daily snapshots versus 28-day deltas as toggleable layers
  • Notification thresholds tied to artist watchlists
  • Playlist + radio + chart as a unified momentum stack

Spot On Track

Playlist monitoring

Reference tool · Playlist specialist

Why operators study this UI

Spot On Track is the narrowest of the three — laser-focused on playlist add/drop events. Its UI is a masterclass in dense row-based catalog tables that still feel readable.

  • Catalog as the primary view — not artists or playlists
  • Add/drop events as a single chronological feed
  • Editorial vs. algorithmic playlist split, color-coded
  • Per-track follower delta as a default visible column
