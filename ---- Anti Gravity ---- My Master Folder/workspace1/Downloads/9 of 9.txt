# Phase 2: Spotify Deep Dive (The Algorithmic Landscape) (Cont.)

This phase concludes the in-depth look at Spotify’s internal mechanics, focusing on the strategic 12-month release plan designed to maximize stream compounding and algorithmic growth.

## Class 204: A Perfect Strategy: The 12-Month Release Plan

**Class Description**
This class introduces the definitive strategy for sustained algorithmic growth: the **Waterfall Release Method**. Students learn how to structure a year's worth of content, release singles consistently (every four to eight weeks), and leverage the crucial technical requirement of **ISRC code reuse** to ensure that stream counts compound and momentum builds exponentially over the long term (18–24 months).

**Success Steps / Tool / Guide (Closed Loop)**

**Waterfall Plan Generator & ISRC Code Tracker:** A structured template for mapping out a 12-month release schedule, detailing when singles should be bundled into larger EPs or albums. The companion tracker emphasizes the critical necessity of **reusing the exact same ISRC code** for older tracks when they are re-released in bundles to compound streams.

**All the Details You Know I Want**

*   **Problem Solved:** Establishing a consistent cadence, touchpoints, story arc, and content-release alignment for an entire year to build momentum.
*   **Success Steps/Tool Implementation:**
    *   **Consistent Cadence:** Release a new single every **four to six weeks**. This consistent rhythm is necessary to jump-start the algorithm, with exponential growth typically triggering around the **18 to 24 month mark**.
    *   **The Waterfall Method:** Release a single (Release A, Song 1). The next month, release a multi-track EP (Release B) that includes the new song (Song 2) **and** the previous song (Song 1).
    *   **Data Stacking:** The re-releases must use the **same ISRC codes** (International Standard Recording Code). Reusing the ISRC code ensures that all streams, saves, and playlist inclusions stack and compound.
    *   **Forward Planning:** Think about planning the **tenth single** while releasing the first to create a cohesive, long-term strategy.

# Phase 3: Financial & Media Strategy (Budgeting and Credibility)

This phase establishes financial discipline and introduces the strategic value of earned media (press) as a tool for increasing algorithmic authority and digital credibility.

## Class 302: How to Budget (Step 1): Initial Testing Budget & Conversion Ads Setup

**Class Description**
This foundational budgeting class introduces the concept of setting a small, consistent weekly budget (starting at $5–$20 per day) to conduct essential **Conversion Campaign** testing on Meta (Facebook/Instagram). The goal is to maximize the acquisition of high-quality listener data from desirable countries (Tier 1/2) that will signal to the Spotify algorithm that the music is worth promoting.

**Success Steps / Tool / Guide (Closed Loop)**

**Conversion Campaign Blueprint (Meta Ads) & Tier 1/2 Country List:** A step-by-step guide to setting up a Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Conversion Campaign, optimizing for the **View Content event** on the landing page. This tool emphasizes targeting a specific list of **Tier 1/2 countries** (e.g., US, Canada, UK, Australia) that yield higher quality fans and have a greater likelihood of conversion than low-priority markets.

**All the Details You Know I Want**

*   **Problem Solved:** Planning out your first month and starting with a sustainable budget to acquire high-quality streams.
*   **Success Steps/Tool Implementation:**
    *   **Campaign Type:** Use **Conversion Campaigns** optimized for a specific event (like View Content) over simple Traffic campaigns to ensure higher quality traffic is sent to Spotify.
    *   **Targeting:** Target **Tier 1/2 countries** that spend money online and have Spotify access. This yields real people, not bots and server farms.
    *   **Budgeting:** While you can start as low as $5 a day, front-loading the budget and spending hard in the **first three days** of a release is advised to quickly boost the song's Popularity Score.
    *   **Testing Ad Creative:** Commit to testing **at least three different video ads** (e.g., verse, chorus, drop) with the budget, as different sections of the song perform differently.

## Class 304: Why Press? Google Card, Spotify SEO, and General SEO.

**Class Description**
This class defines the strategic value of earned media (press releases and articles) beyond simple visibility. Artists learn how successful press coverage improves **Search Engine Optimization (SEO)**, creates a professional digital footprint (like a Google Card), and provides crucial evidence of **momentum** that strengthens pitches to gatekeepers, including Spotify editorial curators.

**Success Steps / Tool / Guide (Closed Loop)**

**Press Asset Checklist & Editorial Pitch Reference Guide:** A checklist detailing the necessary components of a professional pitch (Punchy press release, high-resolution photo shoot images). The guide specifically instructs artists on how to include press mentions and their promotional plan in their **Spotify Editorial Pitch** to editors and other curators to justify why the artist deserves attention *right now*.

**All the Details You Know I Want**

*   **Problem Solved:** How articles improve Google results, credibility, and algorithmic authority (Spotify SEO/Algo).
*   **Success Steps/Tool Implementation:**
    *   **Pitching Context (Why Now):** Virtually every music publication and DSP editor (including Vevo and Spotify) is looking for the **"Why right now?"** answer when considering coverage. Press provides proof of momentum.
    *   **SEO Benefit:** Press coverage helps improve general **SEO** and digital discoverability.
    *   **Timing:** Artists often face a dilemma when sending out press: some outlets want the music out already, while others require weeks of lead time. Start researching contacts and sending the press release early to introduce yourself.
    *   **Assets:** Press releases should be **short** and **punchy**. The music video should generally be the **biggest event** and is often reserved to come out *after* the initial single release (post-release day).
