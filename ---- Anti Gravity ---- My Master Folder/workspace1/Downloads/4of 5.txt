# Phase 2: Spotify Deep Dive (The Algorithmic Landscape)

This phase delves into the mechanics of the music business's most crucial platform, detailing how different playlists function, which performance metrics matter most to the algorithm, and how to avoid catastrophic mistakes like engaging with artificial streams.

## Class 201: Understanding Playlisting: Start to Finish

**Class Description**
This foundational Spotify class differentiates between the three main types of playlists—Editorial, Algorithmic, and User-Generated—which together drive the majority of music consumption and discovery on the platform. Students will learn the mechanics of the crucial **Release Radar** and discover the tools needed to pitch their music directly to human curators.

**Success Steps / Tool / Guide (Closed Loop)**

**Playlist Pitch Navigator & Marquee Access Portal:** A visual guide that clearly separates the three playlist types (Personalized Algorithmic, Editorial, Radio/Autoplay). This includes navigation to the **Spotify for Artists Pitching Tool**, which must be used to submit music to Editorial Curators. The class also introduces the Marquee tool, which alerts existing listeners and followers about new releases.

**All the Details You Know I Want**

*   **Problem Solved:** How editorial, algorithmic, and user playlists work together to maximize stream generation and fan acquisition.
*   **Success Steps/Tool Implementation:**
    *   **Editorial Playlists:** These are curated by human editors. To be considered, music must be pitched via the Spotify for Artists tool at least **seven days** in advance. The ideal lead time recommended by Spotify is **four to five weeks**.
    *   **Algorithmic Playlists:** These are personalized and highly powerful. They include **Release Radar** (for followers) and **Discover Weekly** (for new listeners). Discover Weekly and Radio are consistently among the top discovery sources.
    *   **Release Radar Guarantee:** Pitching a track at least seven days in advance through Spotify for Artists ensures that the song will be added to the Release Radar of all the artist's followers.
    *   **Marquee Tool:** Artists can purchase campaigns through this tool to target listeners who have already engaged with their music on Spotify, driving immediate streams upon release.

## Class 202: Playlisting – University Studies (Deep Understanding)

**Class Description**
This highly analytical class dissects the key algorithmic signals that Spotify uses to determine a song's **Popularity Score** and potential for growth. Students will learn which behaviors (saves, skip rate, shares) are most valuable to the algorithm and how to strategically use advertising to generate the high-quality data necessary to trigger placement on algorithmic playlists like Discover Weekly.

**Success Steps / Tool / Guide (Closed Loop)**

**Algorithmic Signal Tracker:** A dashboard/checklist for monitoring critical metrics derived from Spotify for Artists and third-party tools. This tracks the **Save Rate** and **Popularity Score**. The tool includes a guide on how to front-load budget and drive streams hard in the first **three days** to maximize these signals.

**All the Details You Know I Want**

*   **Problem Solved:** Understanding metadata, save rate, skip rate, listener behavior, and release timing to influence the algorithm.
*   **Success Steps/Tool Implementation:**
    *   **The Save Rate:** **Saves** are an incredibly important metric; they are listed alongside streams and listeners in Spotify for Artists. When a listener saves a song to their library, they are much more likely to listen to it long-term.
    *   **Popularity Score Threshold:** A song must typically achieve a Popularity Score of **around 30%** to begin consistently landing in the valuable Discover Weekly playlists. Boosting ads on Wednesday and Thursday can sometimes push a score over the necessary threshold for a release radar bump on the following Friday.
    *   **Listener Quality:** The algorithmic playlists reward **high-quality fans** who exhibit genuine listening intent (not bots). This is why a low cost per conversion on Facebook Ads, combined with a high save rate, is the winning strategy.
    *   **Front-Loading Budget:** Spending the majority of the marketing budget in the **first three days** of a release is advised to quickly get the popularity score up and ride the algorithmic wave.

## Class 203: Spotify Bots: Let’s Bust Myths and Clear the Air

**Class Description**
This critical class focuses on maintaining the integrity of an artist's data pool. It explains the financial and career risks associated with purchasing **fake streams, bots, or low-quality placements** (artificial streaming). Artists learn to identify the signs of a compromised algorithmic profile and how to use legitimate curation services that rely on human review instead of guaranteed placement.

**Success Steps / Tool / Guide (Closed Loop)**

**Fraud Avoidance Checklist & Data Health Monitor:** A resource that lists the signs of malicious or fraudulent playlist promotion and emphasizes why artificial streaming is detrimental. The **Data Health Monitor** guides the artist to check the **Fans Also Like** section within Spotify for Artists, where the presence of completely irrelevant artists is a signal of data contamination. This tool also provides links to legitimate submission platforms.

**All the Details You Know I Want**

*   **Problem Solved:** How to identify fake activity, how to avoid it, and how Spotify detects music that does not reflect genuine user listening intent.
*   **Success Steps/Tool Implementation:**
    *   **Data Contamination:** Getting placed on user-generated playlists that contain bots will **"fuck up our algorithm"** and "ruin everything". This is because the platform can no longer accurately determine who the artist's real fans are.
    *   **Consequences of Fraud:** Artificial streaming, including the use of bots and scripts to manipulate chart performance, risks having your music removed from the platform.
    *   **Vetting Services:** Differentiate between legitimate services (like **SubmitHub** and **Playlist Push**) that give artists access to individual human curators, versus services that promise a guaranteed number of streams for a flat rate. SubmitHub allows users to filter curators based on genre match, quality, and influence.
    *   **Data Check:** If irrelevant artists start appearing in the **"Fans Also Like"** section, it indicates that the algorithm has been skewed by bad data.
