Critical Analysis

I Take Back Everything I Said About Spotify Marquee

By Brian Hazard • Passive Promotion • April 2024
🧪 NEW FINDING

When Spotify launched Marquee, it felt like the holy grail of music promotion—even better than Meta ads. But after 28+ campaigns and a recent expansion of targeting options, the verdict has shifted.

The Fatal Change

Spotify removed the ability to target previous listeners exclusively. Today, you are often forced into massive 55M+ audiences, diluting the "warm audience" power that made Marquee special.

Previously, I could target an audience as small as 2.1K listeners. Now, a custom audience of 90K in the US is deemed "too small," forcing artists to reach cold audiences at Marquee's premium prices.

The $500 Experiment Results

On a recent campaign, Spotify only spent 10% of a $500 budget. Even with high potential, the delivery engine is failing to reach the people who actually care.

$0.60 Cost / Listener
15% Intent Rate
1.4 Streams / Listener
10% Budget Spent

Marquee vs. Meta Ads

Meta ads win by a mile. At $0.60 per listener, Marquee is failing the value test. Meta ads offer platform diversity, Shazams, the ability to retarget, and a much lower cost per conversion.

Marquee and Showcase are becoming indistinguishable. While Showcase blows through budget faster, Marquee seems to be hit by high demand and limited supply, resulting in poor delivery and accidental "worthless" clicks at $0.35/pop.

🧪 THE STRATEGY

Reserve Marquee and Showcase for albums, where the lifetime value of a listener is highest. For singles? Stick to Meta ads unless you have the budget to burn for experiment.
